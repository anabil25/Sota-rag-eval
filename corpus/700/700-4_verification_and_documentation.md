700-4          VERIFICATION AND DOCUMENTATION

To receive [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program benefits, a family must meet multiple eligibility requirements. An applicant must claim on the application form and in an interview with a [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance-contracted fee agent or caseworker that the eligibility requirements are met. In addition, the applicant may be required to verify that the family or a member of the family meets certain factors of eligibility.

Verification can be obtained through documents, [EIS](#)Eligibility Information System or online system information, contacts with third parties, or home visits, and in some circumstances a client statement can be used to establish the accuracy of information stated on the application or provided by the applicant.

Information listed on the application that would cause the client to be ineligible for the program must be confirmed prior to completing the eligibility determination.

Example:

River Song's application shows a balance of $4,000 in their family checking account. At face value, it appears the Song household is ineligible for benefits because their resources are over the allowable limit. Before denying the application, the caseworker must verify if the funds in the account are countable or exempt.

Verification may be a verbal statement from the household confirming their resources, with enough information for the caseworker to determine allowable exemptions, e.g. source of funds, date of receipt, gross amount of original deposit, amount of retained funds. If unable to get verbal confirmation from the household, a pend notice must be sent requesting this information.

Exception:

Countable income must be verified following [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 756](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/756/756_financial_need__income.htm). If the caseworker is unable to verify income using documents provided or collateral contact, a notice must be sent to the household requesting this information.

700-4 A.      VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

1. Mandatory Verification

The following eligibility requirements must be verified to determine a family's eligibility for [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program:

- Alien Status, for individuals who are not US Citizens;
- Caretaker Relationship, except as noted in [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 712](../712/712_caretaker_relatives.md);
- Child Support Cooperation or Claim for Good Cause (see [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 723](../723/723_penalties.md) for policy on verification of a Claim for Good Cause for not cooperating with [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division);
- Minor Parent's:
  - Living Arrangement, and
  - School Attendance.
- Development of Income;
- Physical or Mental Inability to Work;
- Gross Non-Exempt Income;
- The expected date of birth for the baby in a Third Trimester Pregnant Woman case;
- Changes that may affect the family's eligibility for [TA](#)Temporary Assistance; and
- Approved conditions for ineligible drug felon participation, as outlined in [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 770-7;](../770/770-7_ineligible_drug_felons.md) and
- Questionable Information, see [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 700-4B](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/700/700-4_verification_and_documentation.htm#700-4_b._questionable_information).

Note:

Physical or mental ability to work only needs to be verified to allow an exemption from work activities, exempt someone from pursuing [UIB](#)Unemployment Insurance Benefits, or to allow the two-parent payment standard.

2. Verification Required Prior to Allowing Deductions

The following must be verified to be included in the calculation of the family's [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program benefit:

- Work related expenses (self-employment costs of doing business and child or dependent care expenses);
- Child support payments;
- Shelter costs;
- Deemed parent and step-parent deductions (child support or dependent allowance).

Note:

If obtaining verification of the expense, including a shelter cost, would delay processing the initial application, the household may choose to have the application processed without the deduction. Document this request in the case record.

See [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 705-4A](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/705/705-4_verification.htm#705-4_a._verification_required_prior_to_allowing_deductions) for additional information regarding verifications required prior to allowing deductions.

700-4 B.      QUESTIONABLE INFORMATION

Questionable information is unclear or inconsistent information that contradicts a previous application, written or verbal statement made by the applicant, or other information received by the agency. For example, information would be considered questionable if a family's expenses continually exceed their monthly income.

If information on the application appears to cause ineligibility, it must be verified prior to denial. See [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 700-4](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/700/700-4_verification_and_documentation.htm).

The applicant has the primary responsibility for providing documentary evidence to support statements made, and to resolve questionable information. The caseworker must place verification in the case file or document the verification in the case notes.

Any information that a caseworker determines to be questionable must be verified when it affects a family's eligibility for [TA](#)Temporary Assistance or the amount of the family's [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program benefit. The caseworker applies professional expertise and exercises prudent judgement to determine if the proof is sufficient to document the current eligibility requirement. A family cannot be found eligible for [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program if the applicant refuses to allow the caseworker to verify or document information relevant to the eligibility determination. Verification of previously verified information cannot be required, unless the caseworker determines the information has become questionable.

When information from another source contradicts statements made by the applicant, the applicant must be provided an opportunity to clarify and verify their circumstances.

700-4 C.      DOCUMENTATION

Documentation is a record of what elements are verified and how, and why certain decisions are made that relate to a determination of eligibility for [TA](#)Temporary Assistance. Documentation also supports actions taken while case managing a family to become self-supporting.

The caseworker must document each action taken that is related to a family's application for or receipt of [TA](#)Temporary Assistance. See Administrative Procedures Manual for documentation standards.

Employment verification received from a Work Services case manager is acceptable for use in the [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program budget and does not require additional verification, if all information needed to create a budget is received.
