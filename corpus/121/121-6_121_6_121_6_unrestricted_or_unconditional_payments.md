121-6          UNRESTRICTED
OR UNCONDITIONAL PAYMENTS

Payments cannot be restricted or conditional. A
payment, to be unrestricted, must consist of an unconditional
transfer and delivery of money to the payee.

Unrestricted payments are those which the payee
is free to spend as he or she chooses. The payment goes to the payee as
a sum of money to be spent as the payee determines will best meet the
recipient's need.

If a DPADivision of Public Assistance
staff member has reason to believe that ATAPAlaska Temporary Assistance Program
payments are not being used by a recipient to meet the needs of the child(ren),
and the result of this suggests the possibility that child abuse or neglect
is occurring or likely to occur, he or she must file a report with the
Office of Children's Services (OCSOffice of Children's Services).
