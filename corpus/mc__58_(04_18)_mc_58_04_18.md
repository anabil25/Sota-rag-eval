STATE OF ALASKA

Department of Health and Social Services

Division of Public Assistance

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER: MC #58

MANUAL: Alaska Temporary Assistance Program

DATE: April 2, 2018

The Self-Employment Annualization Standards have been updated for 2018 based on the change in federal poverty level for Alaska.

Highlights of these policy revisions are described below. If you have any questions, please contact the Policy and Program Development Team at [dpapolicy@alaska.gov](mailto:dpapolicy@alaska.gov).

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

[MS 700-4 A](../700/700-4_verification_and_documentation.md#700-4_A._VERIFICATION_REQUIREMENTS)      VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

- Added clarification that proof of compliance with drug felon criteria is required.

[MS 758](../758/758_unearned_income.md)      UNEARNED INCOME

- Added bullet to include Crowdfunding as countable unearned income.

[MS 758-9](../758/758-9_refugee_cash_assistance_payments.md)      CROWDFUNDING

- Added subsection explaining how to count Crowdfunding.

MS 759-6 E (4)      ANNUALIZATION DETERMINATION

- Updated the Self-Employment Annualization Standards due to [FPL](#)Federal Poverty Limit changes effective April 2018.

[MS 770-3](../770/770-3_minor_parents.md)      MINOR PARENTS

- Changed terminology from "teen parent coordinator" to "minor parent coordinator" for consistency.

[MS 770-3 A (1)](../770/770-3_minor_parents.md#770-3_A._DEFINITIONS)      DEFINITIONS

- Added a bullet clarifying that a minor parent is not claimed as a dependent on their parent's open Temporary Assistance case.

[MS 770-3 D (1)](../770/770-3_minor_parents.md#770-3_D._REFERRAL_TO_MINOR_PARENT_COORDINATOR)      REFERRAL TO MINOR PARENT COORDINATOR

- Clarified the [FSSP](#)Family Self-Sufficiency Plan and referral process for minor teen parents.

[MS 770-7](../770/770-7_ineligible_drug_felons.md)      INELIGIBLE DRUG FELONS

- Changed hyperlink in the note from Admin Manual Section 105-1 to [TA](#)Temporary Assistance MS 700-4 A (1) for clarity.

[MS 791-3 (7)](../791/791-3_adequate_notice.md)      ADEQUATE NOTICE

- Clarified the use of adequate notice in loss of contact situations to match state regulations, for consistency.
