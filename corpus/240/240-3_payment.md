240-3         PAYMENT

A. PAYMENT LIMITATIONS

The GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program will pay vendors for certain funeral and burial expenses.  In this section, a vendor means an individual or business that provides funeral and burial services.

To determine allowable burial costs, a vendor must complete and submit a [GEN 61](#)General Relief Assistance Burial Pre-Authorization and Billing Form form.  Without a completed [GEN 61](#)General Relief Assistance Burial Pre-Authorization and Billing Form, allowable burial costs cannot be determined.

GRAGeneral Relief Assistance will only pay for the following funeral and burial services:

B. BASIC SERVICES

GRAGeneral Relief Assistance will pay for basic funeral and burial services provided in Alaska at the rates published in the vendor's general price list, up to a maximum payment of $1,250 to each vendor for each deceased person.

Basic funeral and burial services include:

1. Preparation and embalming;
2. Provision of a cloth-covered casket with at least four handles and a padded and lined interior, which is similar to Sound Casket Company No. 199 gray domet or Puget Sound Casket Co. No. 0 gray flannel square;

**Note:**

This also includes a casket rental used for viewing prior to cremation.

1. One chapel service at the vendor's place of business;
2. Use of a hearse; and
3. Use of other facilities and equipment for burial or cremation if the next-of-kin requests cremation.

C. OTHER SERVICES

In addition to the payment for basic funeral and burial services listed in section [240-3B](240-3_payment.md#240-3_B._BASIC_SERVICES) above,GRAGeneral Relief Assistance will also pay for additional services or expenses provided in Alaska as follows:

1. The cost of the least expensive municipal or private cemetery plot that is reasonably available in the locality where the deceased is to be buried.

**Note:**

Grave markers are part of the cost of a cemetery plot if they are required by the cemetery.  If the cemetery does not require grave markers, they are not an allowable expense.

1. The cost of cremation if the next-of-kin requests cremation.  Allowable cremation expenses include an urn and a special container used to house the body before cremation.
2. The reasonable cost of opening and closing the grave.
3. The cost of a hermetic sealer, oversize casket, clothing, extraordinarily lengthy storage (including the cost of transporting the deceased person to and from the storage facility), and other extraordinary expenses necessary because of special    circumstances, if the Division authorizes the service before the service is rendered.

Note:

[GRA](#)General Relief Assistance will only cover extraordinary lengthy storage costs for remains that are considered "unclaimed" and go through the required court ordered burial process.  The purpose of this expense is to pay for costs associated with storing the remains while the court ordered burial process is completed.  The extraordinary lengthy storage cost is pre-authorized by Policy at the time the funeral home files the court ordered burial paperwork with the Alaska Court System.

The rate at which [GRA](#)General Relief Assistance will pay extraordinary lengthy storage costs is $15 a day and will be pre-authorized by Policy for 60 days at a time.  If the court ordered burial process isn't completed within the 60-day time frame, the funeral home will request an additional storage pre-authorization from Policy.

When the court ordered burial process is complete, the funeral home will submit to [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance a completed [GEN 61](#)General Relief Assistance Burial Pre-Authorization and Billing Form with Policy pre-authorization of extraordinary lengthy storage costs, and the court ordered burial documents for a determination of eligibility.

[GRA](#)General Relief Assistance will only pay the cost of extraordinary lengthy storage from the date Policy pre-authorizes the cost to the date the [GEN 61](#)General Relief Assistance Burial Pre-Authorization and Billing Form and approved court order burial is received at the eligiblity office, for a determination of eligibility.

Example:

At the request of the funeral home, Policy pre-authorizes extraordinary lengthy storage costs on 9/23/21 to pay storage costs from 9/21/21-11/19/21 for 60 days at $15 a day for a total of $900.  On 12/1/21, additional storage costs were requested by the funeral home and pre-authorized by Policy for an additional 60 days from 12/1/21-1/29/22 for a total of $1,800.  The funeral home receives court ordered burial documents on 12/20/21.  The funeral home submits the completed [GEN 61](#)General Relief Assistance Burial Pre-Authorization and Billing Form, with Policy pre-authorization, and the court ordered burial documents to [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance.  [GRA](#)General Relief Assistance will pay extraordinary lengthy storage costs from 9/21/21-11/19/21 and 12/1/21-12/20/21 for a total of 80 days at $15 a day for a total of $1,200.

1. The reasonable cost of transporting the deceased within Alaska from the place of death to a place of burial in a different locality, if transportation is requested by the next-of-kin and it is less costly to transport the deceased than to provide for burial at the place of death or transportation of the deceased from the place of residence to the place of death was at the expense of the Department of Health.

Note:

If the family is requesting that DPADivision of Public Assistance pay to transport the deceased for burial rather than burying them in the city of residence because it is less expensive, it is important that the caseworker find out the final burial plans when the deceased arrives in the city of burial.

If another funeral home will also be requesting GRA funds from DPA to complete the burial, these estimated expenses must also be considered when DPA is determining if it is less costly to bury the deceased somewhere other than the city of residence.

Note:  

The Division of Public Health is responsible for all transportation costs related to autopsies.  GRAGeneral Relief Assistance does not pay for transportation costs to and from the place of autopsy.

1. A processing charge equal to 10 percent of the amount of a payment a vendor makes directly to a cemetery or a provider of transportation for any of the reimbursable services listed in items 1 through 5 above.

Example:

A funeral provider pays $600 to a cemetery for a burial plot for the deceased.  When making payment to the funeral provider, the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance payment will include an additional 10 percent over the cost of the burial plot; the total payment to the funeral provider for the burial plot is $660.

D. OUT-OF-STATE PAYMENT LIMITATIONS

GRAGeneral Relief Assistance will pay out-of-state burial expenses if an individual is sent out of state for medical treatment authorized by Medicaid and then passes away.  Payment is limited to the following situations:

- Burial expenses may be paid at the place of death; or
- If it is requested the deceased be returned to Alaska for burial, GRAGeneral Relief Assistance will pay the reasonable costs of transporting the deceased from the place of death to the place of burial.

Note:  

The $1,250 payment limitation for basic funeral and burial services does not apply to burial assistance provided outside of Alaska.

Note: 

Items 4 and 5 of this section must be pre-authorized by Policy, prior to the eligibility office receiving the [GEN 61](#)General Relief Assistance Burial Pre-Authorization and Billing Form for a determination of eligibility.  If the burial item is not pre-authorized, the expense will not be covered.

E. PAYMENT METHOD

GRAGeneral Relief Assistance will normally make a single assistance payment for funeral  and burial services to the funeral provider that prepares the deceased person for burial or cremation.

However, if the services of one funeral provider are necessary at the place of death, and the services of another funeral provider are necessary at the place of burial, GRAGeneral Relief Assistance will make separate assistance payments to each funeral provider.  Each funeral provider may be paid up to $1,250 for basic services listed in section [240-3B](240-3_payment.md#240-3_B._BASIC_SERVICES) above.  However, when payment is made to more than one funeral provider for basic services, only one of them may receive payment for a chapel service.

F.  CONTRIBUTIONS

The dollar amount of all donations or contributions will be deducted from the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance burial approval amount.

If anyone pays any portion of a more expensive funeral than what is provided for by GRAGeneral Relief Assistance, eligibility for funeral and burial assistance does not exist.

Example:

If the deceased person’s sister wants to donate a coffin valued at $3,000, eligibility for funeral and burial assistance does not exist because it exceeds the amount allowed for a coffin under GRAGeneral Relief Assistance.
