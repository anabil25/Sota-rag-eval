240-2         SPECIALIZED BURIAL PROVISIONS

Note:

[GRA](#)General Relief Assistance burial measures all resources in the month of death, regardless of when the application is submitted.

A. PUBLIC ASSISTANCE RECIPIENT BURIALS

Certain public assistance recipients who die while receiving assistance will be income eligible for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance burial assistance provided they have no other available means to provide for their funeral and burial services.  Other available means include other personal resources, such as a burial insurance policy or legally responsible relatives who are able to provide for the funeral and burial services.  Public Assistance recipients who qualify for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance burial services under this provision are limited to:

• Temporary Assistance recipients,

• Adult Public Assistance recipients,

• Nursing Home Medicaid-only recipients, and

• Children in state custody who are Medicaid recipients.

Note:

If the deceased was receiving ATAPAlaska Temporary Assistance Program, APAAdult Public Assistance, Nursing Home Medicaid, or OCSOffice of Children Services Medicaid, and has no other means to provide for their funeral or burial services, we do not count the income of the deceased when determining eligibility for burial assistance. We do count the deceased's resources. If the deceased public assistance recipient was living with legally responsible relatives, the legally responsible relatives' income and resources count towards the deceased person's burial assistance eligibility.  Please see [GRA MS 240-1](240-1_non-financial_eligibility.md) for the definition of legally responsible relatives.  

If the deceased was not receiving ATAPAlaska Temporary Assistance Program, APAAdult Public Assistance, Nursing Home Medicaid, or OCSOffice of Children Services Medicaid, we count both income and resources when determining eligibility for burial assistance. This includes Medicaid waiver recipients residing in an assisted living home prior to their death.

Note:

A trust does not protect a deceased individual’s income from counting for burial assistance purposes.  If the deceased individual was using a Medicaid Qualifying Trust to maintain Medicaid eligibility when they died, we must count the deceased individual's gross monthly income and resources when determining eligibility for burial assistance.

B. COURT ORDERED BURIALS

A court may order the burial of a deceased person in two situations:

1. The identity of the deceased person is known, and no one has claimed the body for burial.

2. The identity of the deceased person is unknown, and no one has claimed the body for burial.

The caseworker shall treat a court order for the burial of an unclaimed body as an application for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance Burial.  No other application form is necessary.  The court order automatically establishes eligibility for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance burial assistance, so an interview is not required.

If the caseworker becomes aware of legally responsible relatives, he or she shall inform the relatives of their legal responsibility to reimburse the state for any funeral and burial services provided for by the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program.  Refer to [section 220-1(F)](../220/220-1_non-financial_eligibility.md#220-1_F._LEGALLY_RESPONSIBLE_RELATIVES) for an explanation of relative responsibility.

**Note:**

Although court ordered burials are always approved, having a court order does not automatically make all expenses listed on the GEN 61 allowable.  

The caseworker must review the burial charges and only pay those that are allowable as listed in [GRA MS 240-3](240-3_payment.md).  Any additional services that are not preauthorized by Policy as required, must be denied, even if there is a court order.
