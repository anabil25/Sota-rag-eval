240-5 CLAIMS AGAINST THE ESTATE

The amount paid by the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program for the funeral and burial expenses of a deceased person allows the creation of a claim against the deceased person's estate.  This means that the amount of burial assistance provided by the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program will be allowed as a claim against the estate by the court that has jurisdiction over the distribution of the deceased person's estate.  The court will have jurisdiction over the deceased person's estate if the burial was originally ordered by the court or if the deceased person's estate goes into probate.  The court of jurisdiction is normally the Superior Court nearest to where the person died or is buried.  See [Addendum 1](../addendum/addendum_3_judicial_districts_of_alaska.md) for a list of Judicial Districts in Alaska.

Note:  

In Alaska, the court does not have priority right over the family.  In a situation where the deceased is known, the deceased person's family may step forward and collect the assets.  If they do, then the court would not maintain jurisdiction.  This is especially true if the value of the estate is less than $15,000.  In this situation, the heirs can collect the estate assets without any court involvement at all.

If the case worker becomes aware of legally responsible relatives will collect the assets of an estate, he or she should inform the relatives of their legal responsibility to reimburse the state for any funeral and burial services provided for by the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program.  Refer to sections [220-1F](../220/220-1_non-financial_eligibility.md#220-1_F._LEGALLY_RESPONSIBLE_RELATIVES) for an explanation of relative responsibility.

A. CLAIM AGAINST ESTATE FORM

In situations where the court has ordered the burial of an unclaimed body, the case worker shall send a copy of the court order to [hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov](mailto:hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov).

The GRAGeneral Relief Assistance Policy Specialist will complete a Claim Against Estate form and file it with the court that ordered the burial. The GRAGeneral Relief Assistance Policy Specialist will monitor the claim for payment.

B. DEMAND FOR NOTICE FORM

In situations where a burial is not ordered by a court, but there is reason to believe that the deceased person has left an estate over which a court will have jurisdiction (i.e., the estate will go into probate), the DPADivision of Public Assistance office that approved the burial assistance will notify the policy unit by sending an email to [hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov](mailto:hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov).

The GRAGeneral Relief Assistance Policy Specialist will complete a Demand For Notice form and file it with the court. The GRAGeneral Relief Assistance Policy Specialist will monitor the claim for payment.
