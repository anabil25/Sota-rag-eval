240-4         PRIOR RESOURCES

Individuals are not eligible for [GRA](#)General Relief Assistance if they have a prior resource available to meet the specific need.  Applicants must pursue all prior resources the deceased may be eligible for, even if it will take some time to access the resource.

If a vendor receives a payment from a prior resource for funeral and burial services already provided for by the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program, the vendor must immediately refund to the division any amounts received from the prior resource.

Prior resources for funeral and burial assistance provided by GRAGeneral Relief Assistance include but are not limited to:

A. TRIBAL ORGANIZATIONS / BUREAU OF INDIAN AFFAIRS BURIAL ASSISTANCE

Tribal organizations administer the Bureau of Indian Affairs General Assistance (BIA-GABureau of Indian Affairs General Assistance) program.  Often multiple tribes can contribute to the burial expenses for an individual.  Therefore, when it appears that a deceased person may be eligible for this assistance, the caseworker must refer the applicant to any Tribal Organizations that the individual was affiliated with to apply for and pursue these funds.

Note: 

Verification of eligibility must come from the tribal or [BIA](#)Bureau of Indian Affairs organization.  We cannot take client or vendor statement regarding eligibility and/or the amount received from the organization.

B. SOCIAL SECURITY LUMP SUM DEATH BENEFITS

A lump sum death benefit of $255 is payable on the death of an insured worker to the spouse living with the worker at the time of death or eligible to receive benefits at that time.  If there is no qualified spouse, the death benefit is payable to a child or children of the deceased worker who are eligible for monthly survivor benefits.

The case worker will disregard this benefit amount when determining eligibility and payment amounts for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance burial services. However, it is the responsibility of the vendor to immediately refund the Division of Public Assistance any such payments received from a relative.

C. VETERANS' DEATH BENEFITS

Death benefits from the federal Department of Veterans Affairs and the Alaska Department of Military and Veterans Affairs are considered prior resources for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance funeral and burial assistance.  They are an available resource for the deceased person or responsible relatives, and verification of eligiblity is needed.  Refer the applicant to the Department of Veterans Affairs for application of benefits and verification of eligibility.

The federal Department of Veterans Affairs offers a $150 burial plot allowance to any deceased person who was discharged under other than dishonorable circumstances.  An additional $300 burial benefit is available to disabled veterans with service-connected disabilities or veterans who were receiving a VADepartment of Veterans Affairs pension.

Indigent veterans whose remains are unclaimed are entitled to burial benefits; those dying from service-connected disabilities may receive a $1,100 benefit.  Payments from the federal Department of Veterans Affairs are frequently applied for and received by the funeral provider.

The Alaska Department of Military and Veterans Affairs administers the Veteran Death Gratuity program.  Spouses or personal representatives of eligible veterans may apply for and receive a death gratuity benefit of $750.  Eligibility for this benefit is based on Alaska residency for at least one year and an honorable discharge.  The veteran must be buried in Alaska.

The federal Department of Veterans Affairs considers the state's VADepartment of Veterans Affairs benefit to be a prior resource, and federal benefits are not available if burial assistance has been made for the deceased by the state VADepartment of Veterans Affairs.  However, the state VADepartment of Veterans Affairs does not consider the federal VADepartment of Veterans Affairs burial assistance to be a prior resource.  If the client applied for and received federal VADepartment of Veterans Affairs benefits first, benefits could also be received from the state VADepartment of Veterans Affairs.

D.  PAYMENTS FROM PRIOR RESOURCES

If a vendor receives a payment from a prior resource for funeral and burial services already provided for by the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program, the vendor must immediately refund to the Division any amounts received from the prior resource.
