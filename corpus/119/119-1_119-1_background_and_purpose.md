119-1          BACKGROUND
AND PURPOSE

Three separate federal agencies provide funds
for the primary public assistance programs: Medicaid, Supplemental Nutrition
Assistance Program, and Alaska Temporary Assistance. Federal regulations
require states to carefully determine the administrative costs for each
program. One cost allocation method allowed is the Random Moment Sample
(RMSRandom Moment Sample). The RMSRandom Moment Sample allows DPADivision of Public Assistance
to use statistically valid sampling methods to characterize activities
carried out by caseworkers. This state-designed time sample relies on
a random selection of location, worker, and a specific moment in time.

The purpose of the RMSRandom Moment Sample
is to fairly allocate the costs between federal programs. The accuracy
of the system is critically dependent on the willingness and ability of
each staff person included in the sample to accurately characterize the
program work he or she is performing at the randomly selected moment.

The RMSRandom Moment Sample
system produces a random sampling of staff, offices, and time. This is
referred to as a "sample-set" and is produced on a quarterly
basis. The sample-set includes caseworkers that have direct responsibility
for processing applications and authorizing public assistance benefits
or services. Primarily the Eligibility Technicians, Workforce Development
Specialists, and Social Workers participate in the RMSRandom Moment Sample.
Other staff involved in benefit authorizations also must participate if
added to the list of sampled positions.

The RMSRandom Moment Sample
uses a web-enabled format sent by email for the worker to select the program
"service" and "activity" that most appropriately reflects
what they are working on at a randomly chosen moment. The collective total
of these individual reports determines the proportion of funds charged
to each program or work component. Based on the results of the RMSRandom Moment Sample, the federal agencies reimburse
the state for their share of the administrative costs.

119-1 A.      DISTRIBUTION

Each quarter, the RMSRandom Moment Sample
system generates a "sample-set" for the randomly selected Personnel
Control Numbers (PCNPersonnel Control Number).
The RMSRandom Moment Sample system uses an
email/internet polling process to conduct the sampling. The "RMSRandom Moment Sample Observation" form collects
information on the activity for the selected individual's PCNPersonnel Control Number at the designated time.

The individual will receive an email notification
at the randomly selected time. The email subject line will read: "Random
Moment Sample Notification - IMMEDIATE RESPONSE REQUESTED." The email
notification states that, "you are being sampled" and lists
a specified date and time. The email includes instructions with the web
link to access the observation form.

The worker must complete the online observation
form for the precise date and time listed in the email notification. After
entering all the data in the appropriate fields, the worker must click
on the "Save" button. This returns the completed observation
form to the RMSRandom Moment Sample database.
The RMSRandom Moment Sample system will generate
a message stating, "Thank you! Your sample response has been recorded."

119-1
B.      SCHEDULED OBSERVATIONS AND TIME FRAMES

The observation form should
be completed within 24-hours of the time listed on the RMSRandom Moment Sample email notification. RMSRandom Moment Sample Observations are emailed approximately
5-minutes ahead of the actual sample moment to ensure prompt delivery.
A second email reminder is sent to the worker after 24 business hours
from the sample moment if the observation is not completed. If the observation
remains unanswered a third email notification is sent to the worker and
their supervisor after 36 business hours have lapsed. At this point, the
office supervisor is responsible to either complete the observation form
or ensure that the sampled worker completes it. A no response report is
sent to the supervisor every Thursday of the following week if observation
remains unanswered after the third email notification.

In some rare instances an email notification may
actually generate later than the time listed in the RMSRandom Moment Sample
Observation email message, but will generate before the 24-hour time period.
It is essential to take note of the sample time listed on the observation
form to correctly complete the process.

119-1
C.      USING OUTLOOK'S "OUT OF OFFICE ASSISTANT"

When a worker plans to be out of the office for
a day or more, the worker must activate the "Out of Office Assistant"
email feature in Outlook for the time period they expect to be out. This
will generate an "Out of Office Reply" email message to the
RMSRandom Moment Sample Administrator's Inbox
for any observation forms expected during their absence.

The RMSRandom Moment Sample
Administrator will use the "Out of Office Assistant" text message
information to complete the RMSRandom Moment Sample
Observation form on the worker's behalf. The "Out of Office Assistant"
message should include enough information for the observation to be completed.
For example, "attending SNAP training 11/1/09 through 11/16/09"
or "On vacation 11/1/09 through 11/16/09", etc. If the worker
has set the "Out of Office Assistant" in this manner, the sampled
worker can delete any RMSRandom Moment Sample
email notifications that exceed the 24-hour period.

119-1
D.      RMSRandom Moment Sample
ADMINISTRATOR'S ROLE

The RMSRandom Moment Sample
Administrator monitors the dates, time, and completion of each observation
form. The RMSRandom Moment Sample Administrator
is responsible for the federal sample validation. This process requires
follow-up telephone calls to selected sampled individuals to reconfirm
the data they entered into the RMSRandom Moment Sample
system. This is a federal validation requirement. Follow-up may also be
necessary for individuals who failed to complete their scheduled observation
form, or set the "Out of Office Assistant."

119-1 E.      SAMPLING

The RMSRandom Moment Sample
Administrator determines the PCNPersonnel Control Numbers
that must participate in the sampling. Primarily Eligibility Technicians,
Workforce Development Specialists, and Social Workers will appear in the
sampling. Managers working a caseload or providing program policy clarification
might appear in the sampling. Also, administrative staff responsible for
determining eligibility for Diversion and expedited service programs,
or issuing supportive services, will appear in the sampling.

The RMSRandom Moment Sample
system randomly selects individuals for the RMSRandom Moment Sample
quarterly sample-set by PCNPersonnel Control Number
rather than by a name or job title. The worker in the selected PCNPersonnel Control Number at that moment in time completes
the observation form. For example, if an employee moves into a new PCNPersonnel Control Number, either through a transfer to another
office or promotion, they will receive any RMSRandom Moment Sample
observation forms scheduled for the new PCNPersonnel Control Number
and will not receive the RMSRandom Moment Sample
observation forms for the old PCNPersonnel Control Number.
A worker can only receive observation forms for one PCNPersonnel Control Number.

Note:

Workers should not receive RMSRandom Moment Sample
email notifications on their regular day off (RDORegular Day Off).
The supervisor or worker must notify the RMSRandom Moment Sample
Administrator of the RDORegular Day Off
schedule, moves from one PCNPersonnel Control Number
to another, terminates employment, or different Supervisor.

The RMSRandom Moment Sample Administrator
will update that information within the RMSRandom Moment Sample
system.

The RMSRandom Moment Sample Administrator's
email address is [DPA\_RMS@alaska.gov](mailto:DPA_RMS@alaska.gov)
and can be located on the Department Global Address list.

119-1
F.      QUARTERLY SAMPLE SETS

Sampling is set up by calendar quarter. Once the
Quarterly Sample Set starts, few changes can be made to a sample-set.
Neither adding new staff nor deletion of PCNPersonnel Control Numbers
can occur after the start of the sample set. Modifications can be made
to name, PCNPersonnel Control Number, job title,
supervisor's name, telephone numbers, and email address changes. Workers
who are in the sample group should notify the RMSRandom Moment Sample
Administrator of any changes that need to be made.

Quarterly RMSRandom Moment Sample
sample-sets are:

First Calendar Quarter = January, February, March

Second Calendar Quarter = April, May, June

Third Calendar Quarter = July, August, September

Fourth Calendar Quarter = October, November, December

Additional RMSRandom Moment Sample
information and training materials can be located online at <http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/node/view/279>.
