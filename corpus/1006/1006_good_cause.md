1006           GOOD CAUSE

While a client may not always make a formal request for good cause, they may disclose information to the case manager that indicates a possible good cause claim. Case managers are required to explore and thoroughly document potential good cause reasons with the client before a penalty can be requested. If good cause is identified, the [FSSP](#)Family Self Sufficiency Plan should be updated with another activity that is more appropriate, see [MS 1002](../1002/1002_family_self-sufficiency_plan_(fssp).md).

If the case manager requests a penalty, which may be imposed because no good cause was identified, the [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program client must make a claim of good cause to resolve the penalty. Good cause is specific to each type of penalty. For more guidance on penalties, refer to [ATAP MS 723](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/723/723_penalties.htm) and [WSPM MS 1007](../1007/1007_penalties_and_disqualifications.md).
