806-3        RENEWAL REQUIREMENTS

DPADivision of Public Assistance will renew [MAGI](#)Modified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid eligibility every twelve months. [ARIES](#)Alaska's Resource for Integrated Eligibility Services will attempt to determine continued eligibility using electronic data 60 days before the end of the certification period. This includes checking the reasonable compatibility as outlined in [section 823-2](../823_verification_and_documentation/823-2_reasonable_compatibility.md#823-2_Reasonable_Compatibility). If the case is not in [ARIES](#)Alaska's Resource for Integrated Eligibility Services, this process must be done manually. Federal regulations do not allow us to send a renewal form to the household unless we are unable to determine continued eligibility using the ex parte process.

Individuals who have no income counted on their case and no income is found when checking data sources can only be approved using the ex parte process if we received an application or renewal form attesting to zero income within the last 3 years. After this time frame, a renewal form must be sent to the household for completion to attest to their income.

Note:

Alaska is currently approved for a Medicaid waiver that allows [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance to renewal [MAGI](#)Modified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid eligibility for [SNAP](#)Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program Participants whose gross income as determined by [SNAP](#)Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program is under the applicable [MAGI](#)Modified Adjusted Gross Income income standard for Medicaid eligibility without conducting a separate [MAGI](#)Modified Adjusted Gross Income income determination.

When attempting an ex parte renewal, we compare a beneficiary's gross income, as determined by [SNAP](#)Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, to the applicable Medicaid income standard. If the beneficiary's [SNAP](#)Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program gross income is at or below the applicable income standard, the state would conclude that the beneficiary is income eligible for Medicaid.

If [SNAP](#)Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program gross income is above the applicable standard, we cannot use this waiver and must make a separate [MAGI](#)Modified Adjusted Gross Income income determination.

If continued eligibility can be determined, [ARIES](#)Alaska's Resource for Integrated Eligibility Services will authorize a new certification period and send an automated renewal approval notice. The automated renewal approval notice provides a summary of the information used to determine continued eligibility and advises the household to contact [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance within 10 days if any of the information is not correct.

If continued eligibility cannot be determined, a pre-populated renewal form is mailed 45 days before the end of the certification period.

The pre-populated renewal allows the recipient to change information that is no longer correct or add new information. If there are no changes, the individual may sign and return the statement of truth page only. Each signature page has a unique identifier to indicate they have agreed to the information on the pre-populated form. If the renewal or signature page is not returned, the case must be closed with 10 days' notice of adverse action.

A new certification period may be established if a change is reported that affects eligibility (such as the addition of a new household member) and a full redetermination is completed.

[DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance must make an eligibility decision and mail written notice within 45 days from the date a renewal was received.

If the MAGI Medicaid household is determined eligible, the new certification period begins the month after the last certification period ended.

Medicaid may end before the end of the certification period if a change is reported that affects the household's eligibility.

A MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid case will close with 10 days' notice of adverse action because of one of the following reasons:

1) The renewal was received by DPADivision of Public Assistance but further information was needed to redetermine eligibility. The household was given notice to submit the needed information but did not respond timely.

2) A renewal form was not received, and eligibility could not be redetermined using electronic information.

If the Medicaid case cannot be renewed and closes with 10 days' notice of adverse action, the household has 90 days to submit the requested information. If the household is eligible, the case is reopened. A new application is not required and there is no break in service.

Example 1: Renewal received; MAGI Medicaid closed

Morgan's certification period for Expansion Group Medicaid ends 08/30/23. She turns in her pre-populated renewal on 08/24/23. DPADivision of Public Assistance processes the renewal on 09/03/23 and Morgan is over income for Medicaid. As DPADivision of Public Assistance must give 10 days' notice of adverse action, a closure notice is sent, and Morgan's Medicaid ends on 09/30/23.

Example 2: Renewal not received; MAGI Medicaid closed

Bobby is 19 years old and receiving Under 21 Medicaid. He is working part-time at Wally World and taking a few college courses. He is busy and forgets to submit his pre-populated renewal form for his certification period ending 08/31/2023.The MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income case closes with 10 days' notice of adverse action.

In October, Bobby submits a renewal with verification of income attached. Bobby is still within the MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income income standards and the case is reopened back to 09/01/2023. A new application is not required.

Example 3: Verification received after case closure

A household is receiving MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid coverage for their child (mom and dad were previously determined to be over income for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid for themselves). The household submits a pre-populated renewal form on 8/03/23. It reports that Dad started a new job.

The renewal is pended on 8/04/23 for income verification as the caseworker was not able to verify the new income. The verification was not received by the 8/17/23 due date and the coverage for the child is ended on 8/30/23 as August is the last month of the previously approved CEPContinuous Eligibility Period.

On 10/08/23, [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance receives the verification of income. The child’s case is reopened back to 9/01/23 because the case has been closed less than 90 days. A new application is not required.
