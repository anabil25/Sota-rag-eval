Alaska Temporary Assistance Program Manual

[Click here to see this page in full context](#)

STATE OF ALASKA

Department of Health and Social Services

Division of Public Assistance

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER: MC #52

MANUAL: Alaska Temporary Assistance Program

DATE: August 1, 2016

This manual update reflects changes from the Alaska Legislation with the enactment of Senate Bill 91, a change in Federal Legislation (the WIOA), further clarification of allowable work exemptions, and employability assessment time limits.

Highlights of these policy revisions are described below. If you have any questions, please contact the Policy and Program Development Team at [dpapolicy@alaska.gov](mailto:dpapolicy@alaska.gov).

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

[MS 710-3](../710/710-3_disqualifying_conditions.md)      Disqualifying Conditions

- Corrects the numbering conventions in this subsection, e.g. changes 710-3 (1) to 710-3 A.
- Removes the drug felony information from the second note under "1. Fleeing Felon" ‒ this information was moved to a new subsection in section "F."
- Adds a new subsection "F" for drug felons' potential disqualification and eligibility for [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program.
- Adds a note with cross references to the Administrative Procedures Guide and a new [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program MS 770-7 for Ineligible Drug Felons. These changes were done in response to SB91, which updated the Alaska Statute for the Alaska Temporary Assistance Program.

[MS 719-1](../719/719-1_self_sufficiency_planning_requirement.md)      Self-Sufficiency Planning Requirement

- Adds "ineligible drug felon" to clarify who may voluntarily develop, complete and sign an [FSSP](#)Family Self-Sufficiency Plan.

[MS 719-2](../719/719-2_employability_assessment.md)      Employability Assessment

- Aligns [TA](#)Temporary Assistance MS 719-2 with the Alaska State [TANF](#)Temporary Assistance to Needy Families Plan which states an Employability Assessment needs to be completed within 90 days.

[MS 730-2](../730/730-2_exemptions_from_work_activities.md)      Exemptions from Work Activities

- Updates the note in this section for the caretaker of baby exemption. Clarifies that this exemption is for one parent households "BA" and removes language that is inconsistent with federal regulation.
- Adds a note for the medical reasons to clarify when a Health Status Report form (TA 10) is required to adhere with state regulations.

MS 734-2      Tuition Costs

- Updates the acronym and language from "WIA" to "WIOA" due to a change in Federal legislation.

[MS 753-6](../753/753-6_other_exclusions.md)      Other Exclusions

- Updates the acronym and language from "WIA" to "WIOA" due to a change in Federal legislation.

[MS 757-17](../757/757-17_restitution_payments.md)      Summer Youth Employment and Training Program

- Updates the acronym and language from "WIA" to "WIOA" due to a change in Federal legislation.

[MS 759-5](../759/759-5_workforce_innovation_&_opportunity_act_(wioa)_payments.md)      Workforce Innovation & Opportunity Act

- Updates the acronym and language from "Workforce Investment Act (WIA) Payments" to "Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) Payments" due to a change in federal legislation.

[MS 770-7](../770/770-7_ineligible_drug_felons.md)      Ineligible Drug Felon

- Creates a new subsection for ineligible drug felons to adhere to change in the Alaska Statute for the Alaska Temporary Assistance Program.
- Adds the conditions drug felons must adhere to, to be found eligible to receive [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program benefits.
- Adds a note with a cross reference to the Administrative Procedures Manual Section 105-1.
