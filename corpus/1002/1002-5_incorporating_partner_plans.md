1002-5          INCORPORATING PARTNER PLANS

A [Release of Information](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/e-forms/pdf/GEN-58.pdf) may be necessary to work closely with partner agencies to develop an integrated plan for the client.  See [Administrative Procedure manual 105-8.](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/admin/apm.htm#t=105%2F105-8_release_of_information.htm)

Temporary Assistance clients may be involved with and receiving services from many different government agencies or community-based organizations. They may have individualized plans with these agencies and organizations that address employment or other self-sufficiency goals. Temporary Assistance applicants and recipients may be receiving services such as:

- Employment services from another agency or program (such as the Department of Labor [WIOA](#)Workforce Investment Opportunity Act, the Division of Vocational Rehabilitation, or a Native Employment Works Program);
- Social services from other community and government agencies addressing their or a family member's engagement in treatment, counseling, education,[DV](#)Domestic Violence, housing security or other activities;
- Family preservation or reunification services from the Office of Children's Services or another agency that include assignments and activities to maintaining their children's safety or family integrity; or,
- Court ordered Community Work Services or court dates they must attend.

The case manager requests a copy of and reviews the partner agency plan and incorporates those elements that support employment and increased self-sufficiency into the [FSSP](#)Family Self Sufficiency Plan. Coordination of planning and services for the client with partner agency staff ensures:

- Services are not duplicated;
- Conflicts between agency assignments and expectation of the family are minimized;
- Employment goals and activities are consistent with Temporary Assistance program goals and expectations; and,
- The responsibility for monitoring the participant's activities and progress, and providing supportive services is clearly defined.
