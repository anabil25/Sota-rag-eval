812-3        HOW A CARETAKER COOPERATES WITH CHILD SUPPORT ENFORCEMENT DIVISION

A parent or guardian cooperates with [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division by:

1.  Completing and signing the [Child Support Information form](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/e-forms/pdf/GEN-53.pdf) for each noncustodial parent not living in the home of a dependent child;

2.  Helping to identify and locate the noncustodial parent of each child included in the household;

3.  Appearing at a [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division office when requested to provide information or evidence relevant to the case;

4.  Appearing as a witness in a court or other proceeding;

5.  Providing information or attesting to lack of information under penalty of perjury or unsworn falsification; and

6.  Taking any other reasonable steps to assist in establishing paternity and securing medical support.

If the parent or guardian relative fails to cooperate with [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division in any of these ways and does not establish good cause for not cooperating, a penalty to the parent or guardian will be imposed.
