TRANSMITTAL NUMBER:   2009-02

MANUAL:   Alaska Food
Stamp Program

DATE:   September
1, 2009

As a result of the Food and Nutrition Service approving the waiver of
face-to-face interviews for Alaska, changes have been made to the interview
chapter to remove the requirement to document why the face-to-face interview
was waived.  This change was announced by broadcast on August 4 and
is retroactive to June 1, 2009.

In addition, several changes were made to the semi-annual reporting
chapter to clarify what actions must be taken on the Food Stamp case due
to reports of change for other programs.  In some situations, such
as when verification is not provided for another program and those benefits
end, the Food Stamp case will remain open.

This manual change also includes the new gross and net income limits,
standard deduction for households, excess shelter allowance, and heating
and non-heating utility standards that are effective October 1, 2009.
Minor clarifications and technical changes are also included.

Highlights of these policy revisions are described below.  If you
have any questions please contact the Policy and Program Development Team
at 465-3382 or e-mail [dpapolicy@alaska.gov.](mailto:dpapolicy@alaska.gov)

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

[MS
601-3 A](../../601/601-3.md#601_3_A_WHEN_IS_AN_INTERVIEW_REQUIRED_)  When Is An Interview Required?

1. - Removes the requirement to conduct a face-to-face
     interview.

[MS
601-3 D](../../601/601-3.md#601_3_D_HOW_TO_CONDUCT_AN_INTERVIEW)  How To Conduct An Interview

1. - Changes title from "When Can The Face-to-Face
     Interview Be Waived?"
   - Removes reasons a face-to-face interview can be
     waived.
   - Clarifies interviews can be face-to-face, by phone
     or by correspondence.
   - Clarifies caseworker must conduct a face-to-face
     interview if requested by the client.

[MS
601-3 E](../../601/601-3.md#601_3_E_SCHEDULING_INTERVIEWS) Scheduling Interviews

1. - Changes title from "Conducting Interviews"

MS 604-3 A  Semi-Annual Reporting

1. - Adds note about acting on reports of change made
     by a non-household member on behalf of a household member.
   - Further clarifies when Food Stamp benefits are
     affected by a report of change received for another program.
   - Adds note explaining that no action is needed
     when DKCDenali KidCare staff report making a
     change on the income screens.
   - Adds note regarding notice requirements when a
     household leaves the state or returned mail indicates the household
     is out of state.
   - Removes example concerning UIBUnemployment Insurance Benefit
     alerts since these are no longer received on Food Stamp only cases.
   - Adds titles to examples in order to make the section
     more user friendly.
   - Updates "Example: ATAPAlaska Temporary Assistance Program
     Eligibility is Redetermined”  and "Example: Medicaid
     Eligibility is Redetermined” to clarify that the Food Stamp case
     is not closed when the client does not provide verification required
     for another program.
   - Revises "Example:  Fraud Discovers Misinformation
     Reported at Application” to clarify action can be taken on the
     Food Stamp case when fraud discovers information that should have
     been reported at the interview.
   - Adds "Example: Report from Office of Children’s
     Services” to clarify action to be taken when OCSOffice of Children's Services
     reports a change in household composition.

[MS
605-7](../../605/605-7_permanent_fund_processing.md)  Permanent Fund Dividend Special Processing

1. - Removes specific dates for PFDPermanent Fund Dividend
     distribution.

MS 605-7 B  Case Processing With PFD
Income and Resources

1. - Specifies that item 4, Setting the Food Stamp
     Certification Period, applies to applicant households.
   - Adds reference to MS 605-7 C for policy on certification
     periods at recertification during the PFDPermanent Fund Dividend
     season.

MS 605-7 C  Recertification During PFD
Hold Harmless Period

1. - Clarifies the start date of the certification
     period is the 1st month even when it is aPFD
     HH  month and adds an example.

[Addendum
4](../../Addenda/Addendum_4%2b.md)  Standards and Maximum Allotments

1. - Decreases the standard deduction for households
     of 5 or less to $241 and increases the standard deduction for
     households of 6 or more to $256.
   - Updates the gross and net income limits
   - Updates the heating and non-heating utility standards
   - NOTE:  These changes are effective October
     1, 2009, and will be made via the EISEligibility Information System
     mass change job scheduled for the end of August.
