711-4          WHO MAY NOT BE INCLUDED IN THE ASSISTANCE UNIT

Certain individuals living in the same home as the dependent child must be excluded from the mandatory filing unit and assistance unit. The resources and income of these individuals do not count in determining eligibility or the amount of the grant. These individuals include:

1. Adults who receive [SSI](#)Supplemental Security Income or [APA](#)Adult Public Assistance benefits (Interim Assistance payments are not considered an [APA](#)Adult Public Assistance benefit for mandatory filing unit purposes);
2. Sibling(s) of a dependent child who do(oes) not meet Temporary Assistance age requirements;

Note:

Brothers and sisters include half-siblings but exclude step-siblings.

1. An ineligible alien who is not the dependent child's natural or adoptive parent;
2. Any individual for whom state or Title IV-E foster care payments are being paid. See [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 711-6 C](711-6_the__home_.md#711-6_C._WHEN_THE_HOME_IS_IN_PROCESS_OF_BEING_ESTABLISHED) for information about [OCS](#)Office of Children's Services children returning to the home for a trial visit.

Note:

Children for whom guardianship payments are made may be included in the assistance unit. The treatment of guardianship payments is described in [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 758-7](../758/758-7_guardianship_payments.md).

[ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 750-2](../750/750-2_individuals_excluded_from_the_payment_calculation.md) describes how the resources and income of other excluded members are treated.
