250-1.        

A.  NOTICE OF FINDING FOR RENT & UTILITIES

The Division must render an eligibility decision for each identifiable application for assistance and provide a written notice of finding to the applicant within 30 days from the date  the application is received in the District office.

The case worker is required to provide "adequate" notice of any action taken on the application for assistance. Adequate notice is required for all of the situations listed below:

- Finding of eligibility.
- Increase in benefits if in the same month of eligibility.
- Denial of assistance

"Adequate notice" means the notice must be in writing and include the reasons for the  action, specific manual sections supporting the action, and an explanation of the  person's right to request a hearing.

Example of Adequate Notice:

 "Your application for General Relief Assistance (GRA) received on November 1 is approved.  A check in the amount of $49.00 to pay for your rent for November will be mailed on November 15." GRAGeneral Relief Assistance Manual Section 250-1

Example of Inadequate Notice:

"You are eligible for assistance."

Notice of finding on eligibility determinations for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance should, whenever possible be given to the applicant at the close of the eligibility interview. Whether the notice is hand-delivered or mailed, it must be issued in writing.

When sending a notice to a deceased individual, the caseworker must change the salutation to "Estate of deceased name" when creating the notice.

B. NOTICE OF FINDING FOR BURIAL ASSISTANCE

The Division must render an eligibility decision for each identifiable application for assistance and provide a written notice of finding to the applicant within 30 days from the date of the application is received in the District office.

The case worker is required to provide "adequate" notice of any action taken on the application for assistance.  Adequate notice is required for all of the situations listed below:

- Finding of eligibility.
- Increase in benefits if in the same month of eligibility.
- Denial of assistance.

"Adequate notice" means the notice must be in writing and include the reasons for the action, specific manual sections supporting the action, and an explanation of the person's right to request a hearing.

Example of Adequate Notice:

"The application for Burial Assistance received on November 1, is approved.  A check in the amount of $2500.00 to pay burial expenses for John Smith will be mailed on November 15.  GRA Manual Section 240."

Example of Inadequate Notice:

"Your application for Burial Assistance has been approved."

When sending a notice to a deceased individual, the caseworker must change the salutation to the "Estate of deceased name" when creating the notice.

A copy of the approval/denial must be sent to the funeral home also.
