STATE OF ALASKA

Department of Health
and Social Services

Division of Public Assistance

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER:   2008-02

MANUAL:   Alaska Food
Stamp Program

DATE:   September
2, 2008

The recently enacted Food, Conservation and Energy Act of 2008 (also
referred to as the 2008 Farm Bill) contains several provisions that improve
the Food Stamp Program.  Effective October 1, 2008, the $175/$200
cap on dependent care costs is eliminated, the standard deduction and
minimum benefit are increased, and individual retirement accounts (IRAs)
and college savings plans are excluded resources.

In addition, we expanded the residency policy to allow households who
receive Medicaid from Alaska to continue to get Food Stamp benefits when
they are out of state for medical treatment.  Several minor clarifications
throughout the manual have also been made.

Highlights of these policy revisions are described below.  If you
have any questions please contact the Policy and Program Development Team
at 465-3382 or e-mail [dpapolicy@alaska.gov.](mailto:dpapolicy@alaska.gov)

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

[MS
600-2](../../600/600-2_definitions.md)  Definitions

1. - Updates the federal minimum wage amount that increased
     effective July 24, 2008.

[MS
601-3E](../../601/601-3.md#601_3_E_SCHEDULING_INTERVIEWS)  Interviews Conducted at the DPADivision of Public Assistance
Office

1. - Clarifies that, for Food Stamp applications, a
     scheduled interview is one in which the applicant is given a date
     and time to come in for the appointment.  This requirement
     does not apply to the other programs.
   - Clarifies the timing of the automatic application
     denial action when the Food Stamp application is registered as
     a request for service (RF).

[MS
602-1A(5)](../../602/602-1_a.md#602_1_A_5__Excluded_Household_Members)  Excluded Household Members

1. - Revises note to clarify that the worker must obtain
     documentation that the felony conviction is expunged, not just
     set aside, before finding the individual eligible for Food Stamp
     benefits.
   - Adds definition of expunged to clarify policy

[MS 602-1B](../../602/602-1_b.md)
 Residency

1. - Expands the residency policy to allow households
     who are receiving Medicaid from Alaska and are out of state for
     medical treatment to continue to meet the residency requirement
     for Food Stamp Program purposes.

[MS
602-2A(1)](../../602/602-2_a.md#602_2_A_1__Liquid_Resources)  Liquid Resources

1. - Removes references to individual retirement accounts
     as countable resources.  These accounts are excluded effective
     October 1, 2008.

[MS
602-2B(4)](../../602/602-2_b.md#602_2_B_4__Retirement_and_Pension_Plans)  Retirement Funds

1. - Excludes retirement savings accounts when determining
     Food Stamp Program eligibility effective October 1, 2008.  A
     new EISEligibility Information System resource code is available
     that excludes the accounts for the Food Stamp Program but counts
     them for the other programs.

[MS
602-2B(30)](../../602/602-2_b.md#602_2_B_30__College_Savings_Plans)  College Savings Plans

1. - Excludes college savings plans when determining
     Food Stamp Program eligibility effective October 1, 2008.  A
     new EISEligibility Information System resource code is available
     that excludes the accounts for the Food Stamp Program but counts
     them for the other programs.  The University of Alaska College
     Savings Plan continues to be excluded for all programs.

[MS 602-4C](../../602/602-4_c.md)
 Dependent Care Deduction

1. - Removes the $175/$200 limit on dependent care
     expenses effective October 1, 2008. This change will be made via
     the EISEligibility Information System mass change job scheduled
     for the end of August.

[MS 603-3](../../603/603-3.md)
 Determining the Monthly Allotment

1. - Removes references to the $10 minimum benefit
   - Adds a reference to Addendum 4 that lists the
     three new minimum benefit amounts for Urban, Rural I, and Rural
     II
   - NOTE:  The policy at [MS
     603-3A(1)](../../603/603-3.md#603_3_A_1_Initial_Month_Allotment_Less_Than__10) relating to the initial month allotment being less
     than $10 has not changed.  Eligible one and two-person households
     entitled to the minimum allotment will not receive Food Stamp
     benefits for the initial month if the prorated amount is less
     than $10.

[MS
604-1A](../../604/604-1.md#604_1_A_LENGTH_OF_CERTIFICATION_PERIOD)  Length of Certification Period

1. - Replaces earnings
     with earned income to
     provide consistent terminology

[MS 604-3E](../../604/604-3_e.md)
 Verification Requirements

1. - Clarifies that verification of shelter costs is
     needed when the household reports new shelter costs

[MS
605-1B(3)](../../605/605-1_b.md#605_1_B_3_Budgeting_Countable_Educational_Income)  Budgeting Countable Educational Income

1. - Adds reference to use the business mileage rate
   - Adds IRSInternal Revenue Service website
     to get the current business mileage rate

[MS
605-2D(3)](../../605/605-2_d.md#605_2_D_3__Costs_of_Doing_Business)  Costs of Doing Business

1. - Adds IRSInternal Revenue Service website
     to get the current business mileage rate

[MS 605-3B](../../605/605-3_b.md)
 Expedited Service

1. - Adds note to clarify that the case is not closed
     if the household fails to provide verification of deductible expenses

[MS
605-7](../../605/605-7_permanent_fund_processing.md)  Permanent Fund Dividend Special Processing

1. - Removes reference to the $10 amount when addressing
     the minimum benefit
   - Revises MS 605-7B on process for checking the
     PFDPermanent Fund Dividend Interface and PFDPermanent Fund Dividend website

[Addendum
4](../../Addenda/Addendum_4%2b.md)  Standards and Maximum Allotments

1. - Increases the standard deduction to $246 for all
     households
   - Increases the minimum benefits for eligible one
     and two-person households to equal 8% of the one-person allotment.
      For Alaska, this means there are three minimum benefits,
     one for Urban, one for Rural I, and one for Rural II.
   - Removes reference to the $175/$200 dependent care
     deduction cap
   - Updates the gross and net income limits
   - Updates the maximum Food Stamp allotments
   - Updates the heating and non-heating utility standards
   - Moves Lake & Peninsula Census Area, Bristol
     Bay Census Area, and Dillingham Census Area from the South Central
     Utility Region to the Southwest Utility Region.  This change
     is made to better align communities that have similar utility
     costs.
   - NOTE:  These changes are effective October
     1, 2008, and will be made via the EISEligibility Information System
     mass change job scheduled for the end of August.
