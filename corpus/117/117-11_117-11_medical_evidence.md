117-11        MEDICAL
EVIDENCE

When a hearing involves medical issues, including
those concerning a diagnosis, an examining physician's report, and decisions
made by Disability Determination Services, the administrative law judge
may request that a medical assessment be obtained at the agency's expense.
This assessment will be from an enrolled and licensed provider other than
the individual involved in making the original decision. In some cases,
the administrative law judge may simply request additional information
or a second exam from the client's original medical provider. The agency
representative will consult the administrative law judge for specific
procedures in obtaining this assessment.

When reviewing medical information for issues concerning
denial of disability, the administrative law judge may only review information
already reviewed by Disability Determination Services. Clients have the
option of providing additional information. However, Disability Determination
Services must have the opportunity to evaluate the information before
the administrative law judge makes a decision on the case.
