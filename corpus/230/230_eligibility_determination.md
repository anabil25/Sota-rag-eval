230            ELIGIBILITY DETERMINATION

When making an eligibility determination, the worker must consider the immediate need,  resources, and income presently being received by the applicant. Financial eligibility will exist only if the monthly net income is lower than the need standard.

Before examining an applicant's income, the caseworker must determine the nature of the client's need. This is the logical first step of an eligibility interview. If an interview is not possible, the need must be stated clearly on the application. It must be remembered that GRAGeneral Relief Assistance is basically a last resort program designed to meet immediate, emergency needs. If an applicant's need can be met at no expense to the State through another agency, the client should be referred to that agency. Such an agency would be considered a prior resource.

A discussion of the most common and prevalent prior resources for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance follows in section [220-2D](../220/220-2_resources.md#220-2_D._TYPES_OF_PRIOR_RESOURCES).

If no prior resource exists, the caseworker must next examine the personal resources of the applicant and all appropriate members of the applicant's household.

GRAGeneral Relief Assistance may not be approved if the applicant has a prior resource available and adequate to meet the specific subsistence need, or has personal resources in excess of $500.
