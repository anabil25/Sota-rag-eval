423           ALASKA RESIDENCY

To be eligible for Adult Public Assistance, a person must be a resident of Alaska. This chapter explains the state residency requirements of the APAAdult Public Assistance program.

Note:

If during the application processes [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance finds that an individual has open public assistance benefits of any kind in another state or they are an [SSI](#)Supplemental Security Income recipient with an out of state address, the individual is claiming to be a resident of that other state. Until [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance verifies that the individual has reported their move to Alaska to the other state or [SSI](#)Supplemental Security Income, the caseworker must find their Alaska residency questionable. Once residency is no longer questionable, we can start benefits in Alaska as outlined in the program policy manuals.
