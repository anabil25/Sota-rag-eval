423-4        VERIFICATION OF RESIDENCY

423-4 A.  APPLICANTS

The case worker must verify Alaska residency for all APAAdult Public Assistance applicants. Items that can be used to prove Alaska residency include: rent receipts, utility bills, current Alaska driver's license, telephone book, city directory, and statements from other individuals who are aware of the client's situation. If other sources of verification are not reasonably available, and the individual is homeless or newly arrived in Alaska, the case worker may accept the client's statement of his or her intent to reside in Alaska.

Note:

Available interfaces can be used to verify Alaska residency in the following circumstances, unless questionable:

- [SOLQ](#)State Online Query- if the individual is currently receiving Supplemental Security Income ([SSI](#)Supplemental Security Income) and the residential address is in Alaska
- [DOL](#)Department of Labor and Workforce Development/SAM - if the individual has current wages and the residential address is in Alaska
- INME 9/[PFD](#)Permanent Fund Dividend - if the individual is eligible for the most recent [PFD](#)Permanent Fund Dividend year

423-4 B.  RECIPIENTS

The case worker may require APAAdult Public Assistance recipients to provide proof of Alaska residency if there is reason to doubt their continued residency in the State.
