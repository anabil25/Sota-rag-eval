818                MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid Income

Countable income for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid is determined following IRSInternal Revenue Service rules for countable income and deductions with some exceptions. MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income is the household's AGI or Adjusted Gross Income with the addition of the following: tax-exempt interest, Social Security benefits not included in the gross income, and excluded foreign income.

Once the individual's MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid household has been determined using the chart in [section 817-1](../817_magi_medicaid_household_composition/817-1_construct_a_magi_household_for_each_applicant.md), DPADivision of Public Assistance can determine the total MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid income for the individual. The countable income of the individual and individual's household members determines the total countable income for the individual.

Note:

Income for a tax dependent of any age does not count for the household unless it is over the tax filing threshold. Even if the tax dependent chooses to file taxes, if their income is under the threshold it is not counted for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid.

When using non-filer rules, a child's income is excluded unless it is above the tax filing threshold.

In 2025, the tax filing threshold for tax dependents is $15,750 per year for earned income, $1,350 per year for unearned income, or a combination totaling over $15,300 earned plus $450 unearned.

Example:

A tax dependent earns $8,000 from a part-time job and also receives $1,800 unearned income annually. Although the dependent's earned income is less than $15,750, their unearned income is over $1,350, which causes all of their income to be countable income [MAGI](#)Modified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid.

1.  Add all taxable income (such as wages, self-employment, interest, dividends, rental and royalty income, capital gains, unemployment, alimony, etc.),

2.  Add the non-taxable social security benefits, foreign income, and tax-exempt interest (such as employee savings bonds) into the calculation, and

3.  Subtract allowable tax deductions (such as moving expenses, alimony paid, etc.)

4.  The result is the total MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid income for the individual.

Reference [Addendum 3](../addenda/addendum-3_countable_and_excluded_income_for_magi_medicaid.md) to determine if income types are countable for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid.  Reference [Addendum 4](../addenda/addendum-4_magi_medicaid_income_deductions.md) for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Income deductions.
