750-2          INDIVIDUALS EXCLUDED FROM THE PAYMENT CALCULATION

Certain individuals living in the household, who are not included in the [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program benefit calculation must have their resources and income counted in determining eligibility and amount of the grant. These include:

- An ineligible alien who is the natural or adoptive parent of a dependent child in the assistance unit, including parents who are ineligible aliens because they are sponsored by an agency or organization or are over-resource or over-income because of alien-to-sponsor deeming;
- A parent who is married to the dependent child's stepparent when the stepparent owns countable resources that would cause the entire assistance unit to exceed the [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program resource limit if that parent were included in the filing unit; and
- An individual required to be included in the filing unit while that person is serving a [TA](#)Temporary Assistance penalty period, such as the penalty for non-compliance with an assigned work activity or non-cooperation with [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division.

All of the situations above may be time-limited. When the individual's period of ineligibility ends, or the [TA](#)Temporary Assistance penalty is lifted, the caseworker must reevaluate the situation and redetermine eligibility to reflect the changed situation.
