750            FINANCIAL NEED

To be eligible for [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program, a caretaker relative and dependent child(ren) must be financially needy. Financial need, for a child or family which meets the other eligibility criteria for [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program, exists when all three of the following conditions are met:

- The total amount of countable resources available to the assistance unit do not exceed the resource limit; and
- The total monthly countable income available to the assistance unit does not exceed the standards in [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 775](../775/775_income_eligibility.md); and
- The parent or caretaker relative and dependent child(ren) are not eligible to apply for assistance from a Native Family Assistance Program (NFAP).

Note:

To qualify for an [NFAP](#)Native Family Assistance Program, eligible individuals must live in the area served by that program.

See the resource sections to determine if need exists in relation to resources. See the income section to determine if need exists in relation to income. See [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS 710-4](../710/710-4_native_family_assistance_programs_and_eligibility_for_alaska_temporary_assistance.md) for the policy on [NFAP](#)Native Family Assistance Program eligibility.
