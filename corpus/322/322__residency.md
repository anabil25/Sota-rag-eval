322            ALASKA RESIDENCY

To be eligible for Senior Benefits, a person must be a resident of Alaska.

Note:  

If during the application process [DPA](#)(Division of Public Assistance) finds that an individual has open public assistance benefits of any kind in another state or they are an [SSI](#)Supplemental Security Income recipient with an out of state address, the individual is claiming to be a resident of that other state. Until [DPA](#)(Division of Public Assistance) verifies that the individual has reported their move to Alaska to the other state or [SSI](#)Supplemental Security Income, the caseworker must find their Alaska residency questionable. Once residency is no longer questionable, we can start benefits in Alaska as outlined in the program policy manuals.
