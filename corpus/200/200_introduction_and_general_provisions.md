200             INTRODUCTION AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

Alaska’s General Relief Assistance (GRAGeneral Relief Assistance) program dates back to territorial days when the federal assistance programs were not so extensive as they are now.  As the major assistance programs expanded in scope, the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program became more limited in terms of the amount of financial aid available, qualifying circumstances for eligibility, and case load.  Despite the increase in alternative resources, however, the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program remains an important part of Alaska’s welfare system.  GRAGeneral Relief Assistance provides for the most urgent needs of many Alaskans who are unable to meet an emergency need through other programs such as Temporary Assistance or Adult Public Assistance.

The GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program is designed to meet the immediate basic needs of Alaskans experiencing extreme financial crisis.  Those basic needs include shelter, utilities, food, and clothing. In addition, limited funds for a dignified burial of a needy deceased person may be provided.

The GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program is 100% state funded, and as funds are limited, the program is used as a last resort in providing basic needs to an individual or household.  Only after the applicant has exhausted all other possible resources can GRAGeneral Relief Assistance be authorized.  It is the responsibility of each case worker to exercise prudent judgment in the administration of the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance program to insure that all possible areas of assistance and resources have been explored prior to the approval of GRAGeneral Relief Assistance funds.
