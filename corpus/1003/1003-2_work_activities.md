1003-2          WORK ACTIVITIES

All activities defined and described for Work Services must promote self-sufficiency. Activities assigned during the [FSSP](#)Family Self Sufficiency Plan process must be linked to employment goals or other goals determined essential to helping the family prepare for work or to otherwise reach the highest possible degree of self-sufficiency.

Employment in fields or industries that are not federally legal cannot be used to meet the work activity requirements.  While allowed by Alaska state law, cannabis is not federally legal.  Clients interested in working in the cannabis industry should be redirected to another field of employment during work search or coached to close their [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program case if a job has been accepted.  Work hours may not be entered into the [CMS](#)Case Management System Work Activity Screen.

Clients will not be subject to [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program sanctions for leaving employment that is not considered suitable per [ATAP MS 722-1](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/ta.htm#t=722%2F722-1_suitable_employment.htm).

Core work activities that lead a client to self-sufficiency include: employment, subsidized employment, unpaid work experience, job search, job readiness workshops, application for other social service programs, vocational training, counseling, substance abuse treatment, and subsistence activities.

Clients should be assigned 40 hours a week of work activities. This allows for maximum participation and increases the likelihood that each family will meet the minimum participation requirements to the extent possible. Where 40 hours is not possible or appropriate, activities should be assigned based on the client's situation and the availability of activities in their community. Activities should be chosen to assist the client towards self-sufficiency and closing their [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program case as soon as possible. The reason for assignment of less than 40 hours of work activities should be documented in the [CLNO](#)Client Notes. Work Services activities are assigned and scheduled so that clients can exit Temporary Assistance as quickly as possible. Each [FSSP](#)Family Self Sufficiency Plan identified identifies one or more activities the client is expected to engage in during the duration of that plan, see [WSPM](#)Work Services Procedure Manual [MS](../1002/1002_family_self-sufficiency_plan_(fssp).md)[1002](../1002/1002_family_self-sufficiency_plan_(fssp).md). Clients are required to engage in a single or combination of concurrent activities and may be penalized if they fail to do so and do not have good cause, see [WSPM](#)Work Services Procedure Manual [MS](../1006/1006_good_cause.md)[1006](../1006/1006_good_cause.md) and [WSPM](#)Work Services Procedure Manual [MS 1007](../1007/1007_penalties_and_disqualifications.md).

There are three categories of work activities that are assigned to clients and coded in [CMS](#)Case Management System. These activities assist the client in moving towards self-sufficiency.

- Core activities always count towards the federal participation rate though some of the activities have an annual or life-time limit of months that count towards the participation rate.

- Work support activities count towards the participation rate if the client is also engaged in at least 20 hours per week of core week activities.

- Non-countable activities do not count towards federal or state participation rates, but the activities may be in the best interest of the client to build stability and prepare them for work support or core activities.
