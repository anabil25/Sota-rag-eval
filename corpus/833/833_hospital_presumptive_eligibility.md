833         HOSPITAL PRESUMPTIVE ELIGIBILITY

Hospital Presumptive Eligibility is a requirement of the Affordable Care Act (ACA) that was effective January 1, 2014. States are required to allow approved hospitals to presumptively determine eligibility for certain categories of MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid and Breast & Cervical Cancer Medicaid (BCCM / Ladies First / AK B+C).

HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility can only be used for the following MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid categories:

1)  Children Under Age 19;

2)  Parents/Caretaker Relatives;

3)  Pregnant Women;

4)  Former Foster Care;

5)  Expansion Group; and

6)  Breast and Cervical Cancer / Ladies First (see [ADLTC section 575](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/adltc/525/525_general_medicaid_trust.htm))

833 A.  Eligibility

Simplified Determinations

Hospitals will use a simplified application (HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility 1) as states are not allowed to ask all of the questions that are listed on the full Medicaid application. Hospital Presumptive Eligibility is only temporary coverage. In order to receive ongoing Medicaid coverage, these individuals must submit a regular Medicaid application and be found eligible for Medicaid by the Division of Public Assistance. DPADivision of Public Assistance is authorizing the Medicaid benefits based on the hospital's determination.

The Required Gen 50C

Hospitals will assist individuals that have been approved for Hospital Presumptive Eligibility to complete a paper Gen 50C  for Medicaid and submit it to their local DPADivision of Public Assistance office within the approved HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period. Individuals approved for full MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid will be authorized in ARIESAlaska's Resource for Integrated Eligibility Services, if possible.

The presumptive eligibility period begins the date that the hospital determines the applicant presumptively eligible for Medicaid and ends the earlier of:

1)  The date the ineligibility determination for regular MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid is made, if a full application for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid is filed by the end of the month after the month that presumptive eligibility is determined; or

2)  The last day of the month after the month of the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Medicaid determination, if a full application for Medicaid is not filed by that date.

Variable End Date

The end date for the Hospital Presumptive Eligibility depends on whether or not the individual submits a full Medicaid application during the approved HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility coverage period. If an individual received Medicaid through HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility and a full Medicaid application is received within the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility coverage period, the application must be prioritized and processed immediately to ensure the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period is adjusted as necessary. The local office receiving the full Medicaid application must make sure that the Hospital Presumptive Eligibility ends when it should, which could mean extending or shortening the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility approved period.

- If the individual(s) are determined eligible for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid benefits. The ARIESAlaska's Resource for Integrated Eligibility Services case should start the first day of the month that the application was filed, and we determine that the household is eligible. There is no need to adjust the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility case in EISEligibility Information System. Do not shorten the HPE benefits in EIS when MAGI Medicaid benefits are approved. Retroactive Medicaid requests should be handled normally.

- If the individual(s) are ineligible for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid benefits, the ARIESAlaska's Resource for Integrated Eligibility Services case should be denied and the end date on the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility case in EISEligibility Information System should be shortened to the date the Medicaid eligibility determination was made.

- If the individual(s) eligibility for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid benefits is unknown by the date the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility authorization is expected to end (example: the Medicaid eligibility determination has not yet been made because the case is pended), the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility case in EISEligibility Information System should be extended by one month.

Example:

A GEN 50CApplication for Services is received on 10/21 for Medicaid. The individuals listed on the Gen 50C  are looked up in EISEligibility Information System to see if they received Medicaid under FSOFull Service Office 15. It is found that the individual was authorized HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Medicaid from 09/15 through 10/31. This Medicaid application needs to be prioritized and processed immediately. This is to ensure that a Medicaid determination (approval or denial) on the regular Medicaid application is made before the authorized HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Medicaid ends. If a Medicaid eligibility determination is not made by 10/31, the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Medicaid case must be processed and authorized for another month (this will also require reopening the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Medicaid case). If DPADivision of Public Assistance determines that this individual is ineligible for Medicaid on 10/22, the previously authorized HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period will need to be shortened.

DPADivision of Public Assistance will only alter (shorten or extend) the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefit if the individual submits a full Medicaid application within the approved HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period. Applications received prior to or after the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period will not affect the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefits start, and end date authorized by the hospital.

The Approved Hospital List

Please refer to the "Approved Hospital List" prior to authorizing hospital presumptive Medicaid. This document includes:

- A list of hospitals that are approved to make HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility determinations and their contact information.

- Assigned EISEligibility Information System caseload IDs for each approved hospital and the unit numbers needed to authorize the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility in EISEligibility Information System.

- A list of hospital staff members that have successfully completed the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility training. Only hospital staff members on this list are allowed to make HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility determinations.

If an HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility packet is received from a hospital that is not on the approved list or the Presumptive Eligibility Worksheet (HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility 2) is signed by a hospital staff member not on the approved list, do not approve the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefit until you verify eligibility by contacting policy at [hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov](mailto:hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov).

Note:

There are two approved groups making HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility determinations at Alaska Native Medical Center: Alaska Native Tribal Health Consortium and Southcentral Foundation. Make sure you are using the correct caseload id and faxing the coupon to the correct group.

How the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Application Affects Ongoing Benefits

Information entered by the applicant on the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility application (HPE 1) is information reported to the hospital in order for them to make an eligibility determination. The information is not considered to be a report of change to DPADivision of Public Assistance. We should not use the information showing on the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility application to make case changes to any open program the client may be receiving. Only if the client applies for regular Medicaid using a full Medicaid application should we check for report of change.

The Hospital HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Screening

The hospital staff member must screen the applicant for HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility eligibility. This screening involves the applicant or their spouse attesting to the Medicaid eligibility factors required for HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility. An authorized representative can sign the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility application if the applicant is unable to do so but the applicant or their spouse must verbally answer the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility screening questions. If the applicant is unable to do so, hospitals have been instructed not to authorize HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefits. A GEN 50CApplication for Services should be submitted to their local DPADivision of Public Assistance office for a full Medicaid determination.

An individual can only have one hospital presumptive eligibility approval within a 12-month period, starting with the month of the initial HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period.  A pregnant woman can be found presumptively eligible once per pregnancy, even if the pregnancies occur within the same 12-month period.

Example 1:

An individual received HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefits on 9/8/15. The 12-month period for this client is 9/2015 – 8/2016 so the client is eligible for HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefits again 9/1/2016.

Example 2:

A pregnant woman received HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility coverage starting 09/08/2015. The pregnancy ended, and she becomes pregnant again on 07/02/2016. As it is a second pregnancy, she is eligible for another HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period, even though it has not been a year since the initial HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period.

Example 3:

A woman received HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility coverage for Expansion Medicaid starting 09/08/2015. She becomes pregnant and applies for HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility coverage on 01/05/2016. As the initial HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period was not for Pregnant Woman Medicaid and it has not been 12 months since the approval, she is not eligible for HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility coverage from the 01/05/2016 application.

A qualified hospital is one that participates as a provider under the Medicaid state plan and has notified the Medicaid agency of its election to make presumptive eligibility determinations and agrees to make presumptive eligibility determinations consistent with state policies and procedures. A qualified hospital must help individuals complete a full Medicaid application.

If any approval paperwork is received from a hospital not on this list or the hospital presumptive eligibility determination is signed by a hospital worker not listed, please contact the Policy & Program Development Team at [hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov](mailto:hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov).

HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility MOUMemorandum of Understanding

Each hospital that would like to make presumptive eligibility determinations must indicate their interest by completing a Memorandum of Understanding (MOUMemorandum of Understanding) with the Division of Public Assistance. Interested hospitals that do not already have a HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility MOUMemorandum of Understanding in place should send their request for a MOUMemorandum of Understanding to [hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov](mailto:hss.dpa.policy@alaska.gov)

HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Training for Hospital Staff Members

Once an HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility MOUMemorandum of Understanding has been approved for the hospital, every hospital worker that will make these determinations must complete on-line HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility training created by [SD&T](#)Staff Development and Training and pass a knowledge test prior to any determinations being made.

A list of all approved hospitals and hospital workers that have successfully completed training is available on the DPAweb under the MAGI Medicaid manual at <http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/node/15>.

Client Attestation

Hospitals are not allowed to verify information provided by an HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility applicant. They will take client attestation of specific eligibility factors. If the individual(s) are found eligible for HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefits, the hospital will fax the initial HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility packet to [DOST](#)Division Operation Support Team for processing. If any of these initial HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility application materials are accidentally sent directly from the hospital to a local DPADivision of Public Assistance office for processing, please send them to [hss.dpadost@alaska.gov](mailto:hss.dpadost@alaska.gov) immediately.

The existing information in EISEligibility Information System should not be changed based on a HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility application, with one exception: if the individual does not currently receive public assistance benefits and the only change is a slight name difference (Ex: Daniel VS Danny). Remember to create an alias if making a "given" name change. A HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility application is not a report of change to DPADivision of Public Assistance.

Duplicating Medicaid Benefits

If the individual is found to be eligible for regular Medicaid, there is no need to make any changes to the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility case in EISEligibility Information System, as this should already be closed. When making the Medicaid eligibility determination in ARIESAlaska's Resource for Integrated Eligibility Services, the Medicaid should start the first day of the month that the application was filed, and we determine that the household is eligible. Retroactive Medicaid requests should be handled normally.

Changing (Pulling Back) HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility End Date(s)

When a HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility recipient submits a GEN 50CApplication for Services MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid application prior to the end of their HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period and the client is determined ineligible for MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid, the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility END DATE must be updated on the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility case and pulled-back to the date that MAGIModified Adjusted Gross Income Medicaid ineligibility was determined. This includes if you must deny the Medicaid application because the household did not provide the verification necessary to make an eligibility determination.

Example:

Jon was approved for HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefits from 03/13/18 - 04/30/18. Jon submitted a GEN 50CApplication for Services to his local office on 04/27/18. Since the application was submitted within the approved HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period, it was processed immediately. The application was pended for verification needed to make an eligibility determination that was not due until 05/08/18. HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefits for Jon must be issued for 05/18 since we are unable to make an eligibility determination by 04/30/18. Jon fails to provide the verification, and his Medicaid application is denied on 05/11/18. The HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility benefits must be ended on 05/11/18, the date the Medicaid application is denied.

Newborns

Newborns born to women during the HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility period are not eligible for newborn "BABaby Medicaid" Medicaid as they were not born to women eligible for and receiving Medicaid in the month of delivery.

Exception:

If regular retroactive Medicaid was approved for the mother in the birth month, the newborn would then be eligible for newborn "BABaby Medicaid" Medicaid.

Example:

Providence Hospital determines Sue Johnson presumptively eligible for Medicaid on 09/08/17 in the pregnant woman category and provides Sue with an approval letter. Sue's eligibility for HPEHospital Presumptive Eligibility Medicaid ends on 10/31/17. However, since Sue applied for full Medicaid in the month she gave birth and we issued Medicaid starting 10/01/17 to cover the birth expenses, the baby is also eligible for "BABaby Medicaid" Medicaid.

Requests for Retroactive Medicaid Coverage

If the applicant submits a regular application and requests Retroactive Medicaid coverage, the same rules for retroactive coverage apply. Retroactive months are the 3 months immediately preceding the submission of a regular Medicaid application.

A state may disqualify a hospital from conducting presumptive eligibility determinations if the state finds that the hospital is not making or is not capable of making accurate presumptive eligibility determinations in accordance with applicable state policies and procedures.

833 B. Coverage

Individuals determined eligible under hospital presumptive eligibility are provided the same coverage as those provided under the eligibility group for which hospital presumptive eligibility is determined.

The exception is pregnant women whose coverage is limited under hospital presumptive eligibility and does not cover inpatient hospitalization, including labor and delivery. If the woman is approved for regular pregnancy Medicaid after completion of a Gen 50C , coverage will include these inpatient services.

Questions regarding covered services should be directed to the Alaska Medicaid Recipient Helpline at 800-780-9972.

833 C. Subtypes

The income standards for all MAGI Medicaid categories based on the [FPL](#)Federal Poverty Limit are revised each April. The Parent/Caretaker Relative and Under 21 categories are based on the [COLA](#)Cost of Living Allowance and are revised each January.

\*XP should be rarely used as this subtype is only for individuals who are Non-Newly Eligible as described in [MS 816-1(G)](../816_magi_medicaid_categories/816-1_magi_category_eligibility_factors.md#816-1_G._EXPANSION_GROUP_).
