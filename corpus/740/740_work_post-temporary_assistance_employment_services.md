740            WORK: POST-TEMPORARY ASSISTANCE EMPLOYMENT SERVICES

Although finding a job is a crucial step toward self-sufficiency, staying employed is just as important. Many newly employed [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program recipients struggle with the challenges of employment. Nationally, more than one-third of families who leave public assistance due to employment return within a year.

Job retention services have proven an effective means of helping people who find a job to retain it and to remain off of assistance. These services include:

- Extended case management and supportive services;
- [PASS](#)Parents Achieving Self-Sufficiency II Child Care; and
- Other public assistance programs.

Eligibility for each of these services must be considered separately since each service has different eligibility requirements.
