220-2        RESOURCES

A. INADEQUATE RESOURCES

An applicant must have inadequate or inappropriate resources to meet the specific need.  The applicant must lack the immediate financial means, either in the form of cash on hand or on deposit, personal property which may be readily converted to cash, credit, or eligibility for assistance through other programs, to provide for the specific need.

B. PRIOR RESOURCE

General Relief Assistance may not be granted if an applicant, despite an excess of need over income, has other resources available and adequate to meet the specific need.

In many cases, an applicant's need can be met by other programs or agencies.  The case worker must always check for prior resources before determining eligibility.  GRAGeneral Relief Assistance is considered the last resort.

C. PERSONAL RESOURCES

To be eligible for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance, the household’s personal resources cannot exceed $500.  Personal resources include the following:

1. liquid assets such as cash, savings, stocks, or bonds;

Note:

Unlike other Public Assistance programs, GRAGeneral Relief Assistance burial measures all resources at the time of death even when money is also income received in the same month.  This is true even if a new application is submitted after a previous application has been denied.

2. any real or personal property with equity value, not including:

1. the home in which the applicant resides and the land on which it stands, unless the land has been surveyed for subdivision.  If the land has been surveyed for subdivision, only the lot on which the house stands may be excluded;
2. property actively marketed for sale at fair market value or less; Fair Market Value - is determined by the caseworker by checking with realtors, real estate agents, classified advertisement listings, the tax assessor's office, stockbrokers, or insurance agents, as appropriate in order to determine the probable value of the resource if it were to be sold. If the fair market value of a particular resource cannot be determined exactly, the worker may establish its fair market value by determining the value of comparable property in the vicinity which is being offered for sale, or which has been recently sold.
3. property that is producing reasonable income; Reasonable Income - is determined by checking with realtors, real estate agents, rental agencies, classified advertisement listings, etc., to determine whether the income produced is comparable to that being produced by similar properties in the vicinity. Producing income does not necessarily imply making a profit. Property may be producing income comparable to that normally produced by other property in the same area and yet it may be losing money after deductions for expenses are taken (refer to [section 220-3](income.md)). Any profit gained on income producing property must be considered income to the applicant (refer to [section 220-3](income.md)). However, the property itself will still be considered an exempt resource.
4. property that is essential to employment;
5. either an automobile or a motorcycle or, in an area where local transportation needs require an alternative, a boat, snowmobile, all-terrain vehicle (ATV) or a combination of any two;

Example:

A two-person household owns two cars. One car is used for family transportation and the other car, a taxi, is used to produce income.  Neither car is considered an available resource.

3. A Limited Entry Fishing Permit that was not used during the most recent season for reasons other than:

1. Loss of essential fishing equipment prior to or during the most recent season;
2. Serious illness of the applicant or a household member for the period prior to and including the most recent season; or
3. Lease of the Limited Entry Fishing Permit at fair market value to produce income for the applicant's household;

1. Credit or the opportunity to obtain credit from a vendor who provides the subsistence item for which the household is requesting GRAGeneral Relief Assistance.  (The credit must be granted in time to meet the need, and it must be sufficient to meet all of the specified need.)
2. Crowdfunding Accounts.  Crowdfunding is the practice of obtaining needed funding by soliciting contributions from a large number of people, typically from the online community.  e.g. go fund me.  If the client sets up the account themselves and has access to it, it is countable income in the month received and a countable resource the following month.  If someone else sets up the account and gives the client funds, it is considered unearned income, unless the funds were given directly to the vendor itself.
3. For burial assistance, crowdfunding accounts are intended for burial services and therefore countable towards the deceased individual's resource limit, no matter who sets up the account.

If a household does not meet non-financial eligibility requirements or possesses prior or personal resources that render them ineligible, an income eligibility determination is not required.  The interview can be concluded, and the applicant notified that the application is denied.  If a household meets the resource requirements, the caseworker should proceed with the income eligibility determination.

D. TYPES OF PRIOR RESOURCES

General Relief Assistance may not be granted if an applicant, despite an excess of need over income, has other resources available and adequate to meet the specific need.

In many cases an applicant's need can be met by other programs or agencies. The caseworker must always check for prior resources before determining eligibility. GRAGeneral Relief Assistance is considered the last resort.

The most common resources are discussed below.

1. FOOD STAMP BENEFITS

If an applicant applies for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance for food, first determine his status as a food stamp recipient. If the client is not eligible for food stamps, a food purchase warrant may be issued to a local store.

2. SOCIAL SECURITY DEATH BENEFITS

The Social Security Administration will pay the surviving spouse or children (refer to [section 240-4 B](../240/240-4_prior_resources.md#240-4_B._SOCIAL_SECURITY_LUMP_SUM_DEATH_BENEFITS)) up to $255 for the burial of SSASocial Security Administration eligibles. It is the responsibility of the funeral home to notify and refund to the Division such payments received from a relative.

3. CATEGORICAL PROGRAMS

If a person is eligible to receive benefits from a categorical program (such as Temporary Assistance, APAAdult Public Assistance, or Heating Assistance), this source of assistance must be used before GRAGeneral Relief Assistance. An exception to this is a Public Assistance recipient burial (refer to [section 240-2 A](../240/240-2_specialized_burial_provisions.md#240-2_A._PUBLIC_ASSISTANCE_RECIPIENT_BURIALS)).

4. OTHERS

Other prior resources include but are not limited to: Workers' Compensation, Office of Vocational Rehabilitation, assistance from the Department of Veterans Affairs, Bureau of Indian Affairs General Assistance, and charitable organizations such as the Salvation Army, Red Cross, etc.
