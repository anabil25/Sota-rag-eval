717-1          INFORMING APPLICANTS AND RECIPIENTS ABOUT CHILD SUPPORT ENFORCEMENT AND REQUIREMENTS

When an initial application is received and when a recipient requests that a child be added to the case, the caseworker must discuss child support assignment, cooperation and enforcement requirements with the parent or caretaker relative who are required to cooperate and provide them with:

- A Child Support Form (GEN 53) to complete for each noncustodial parent not living in the home of the dependent child;
- Written notice of the parent or caretaker relative's right to claim good cause for not cooperating with [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division (provided on the [GEN 50C](#)Application for Services and the [GEN 53](#)Child Support Information Form);
- A Child Support Cooperation Statement form ([GEN 80](#)Child Support Cooperation Statement or [EIS](#)Eligibility Information System notice [W060](#)Child Support Cooperation Statement) for a parent or caretaker relative to complete if they choose not to provide the information requested on the [GEN 53](#)Child Support Information Form and want to claim good cause; and
- An opportunity to request [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division keep the family's address confidential because of a threat to their well-being. The "Affidavit and Request for Nondisclosure of Identifying Information" form (CSSD 04-0502) is used for this purpose. This form is used to keep identifying information from being released to a person (such as a noncustodial parent) who would otherwise be entitled to have information about the child support case.

Note:

Cooperation with [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division is also a requirement in an Adult Not Included (ANI) case. If the caretaker relative does not cooperate with [CSED](#)Child Support Enforcement Division the monthly [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program benefit will be distributed to a protective payee until the caretaker relative becomes compliant.

A financial penalty is not placed on an [ANI](#)Adult Not Included case because the income of the caretaker relative is not included in the benefit amount determination.
