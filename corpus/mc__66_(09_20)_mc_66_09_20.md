STATE OF ALASKA

Department of Health and Social Services

Division of Public Assistance

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER: MC #66

MANUAL: Alaska Temporary Assistance Program

DATE: September 1, 2020

The Alaska Temporary Assistance Program (ATAP) heating and non-heating utility standards change effective October 2020. The Eligibility Information System (EIS) will automatically apply these new standards when determining eligibility and benefit amounts beginning October 2020.

Highlights of all the policy revisions are described below. If you have any questions, please contact the Policy and Program Development Team at [dpapolicy@alaska.gov](mailto:dpapolicy@alaska.gov).

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

[MS 701-1 A](../701/701-1_what_is_the_60-month_time_limit_.md#701-1_A._INFORMING_FAMILIES_ABOUT_THE_60-MONTH_TIME_LIMIT)      INFORMING FAMILIES ABOUT THE 60-MONTH TIME LIMIT

- Removed reference to obsolete notice W080 "Work, Time Limits, and Assistance."

[MS 701-1 B](../701/701-1_what_is_the_60-month_time_limit_.md#701-1_B._TO_WHOM_THE_LIMITS_APPLY)      TO WHOM THE LIMITS APPLY

- Clarified the date [TANF](#)Temporary Assistance to Needy Families months became countable toward the 60-month time limit.

[MS 705-3 E](../705/705-3_the_interview.md#705-3_E._SCHEDULING_INTERVIEWS)      SCHEDULING

- Revised bullet #1 to clarify that the individual must agree to the date and time of scheduled interview.

[MS 710-4](../710/710-4_native_family_assistance_programs_and_eligibility_for_alaska_temporary_assistance.md)      NATIVE FAMILY ASSISTANCE PROGRAMS AND ELIGIBILITY FOR ALASKA TEMPORARY ASSISTANCE

- Added a note clarifying that if it is discovered that a family is receiving [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program when they should be served by a [NFAP](#)Native Family Assistance Program, the ongoing case should be closed with 10-day notice of adverse action using the W481 and the case manager notified. Overpayments are not processed in this situation.

[MS 711-2](../711/711-2_mandatory_filing_unit_(mfu)__who_must_be_included_in_the_assistance_unit.md)      MANDATORY FILING UNIT (MFU): WHO MUST BE INCLUDED IN THE ASSISTANCE UNIT

- For better understanding of the policy, added explanation that a biological parent will always be considered a natural parent and a caretaker relative AND will always be a mandatory household member if living with a biological child that has been adopted.

[MS 711-3](../711/711-3_definitions.md)      DEFINITIONS

- For better understanding of the policy, added explanation that a biological parent will always be considered a natural parent and a caretaker relative AND will always be a mandatory household member if living with a biological child that has been adopted.

[MS 712-1](../712/712-1_definition_of_a_caretaker_relative.md)      DEFINITION OF A CARETAKER RELATIVE

- For better understanding of the policy, added a note explaining that natural parents whose legal and financial relationship has been terminated are still considered a caretaker relative and a mandatory household member.

[MS 712-3](../712/712-3_parental_relationship.md)      CARETAKER RELATIVES

- Added blood relation requirement to the "natural parent" definition to differentiate between the "natural" and "adoptive parent" definitions.

[MS 756](../756/756_financial_need__income.md)      FINANCIAL NEED: INCOME

- Clarified that paycheck advances are counted as income in the month received.

[MS 756-3](../756/756-3_definition_of_total_gross_monthly_income.md)      DEFINITION OF TOTAL GROSS MONTHLY INCOME

- For better understanding of the policy, added in-kind income (as described in [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program MS 758-2) to the definition of total gross monthly income.

[MS 759](../759/759_earned_income.md)      EARNED INCOME

- Clarified that paycheck advances are counted as income in the month received.

[MS 770-3 A](../770/770-3_minor_parents.md#770-3_A._DEFINITIONS)      DEFINITIONS

- Removed duplicative language defining an adult-supervised living arrangement.

[MS 780-19 G](../780/780-19_shelter_allowance.md#780-19_G._HOMELESS_SHELTER_DEDUCTION)      HOMELESS SHELTER DEDUCTION

- Revised homeless shelter deduction from $152 to $157.

[ADDENDUM 1](../addenda/addendum_1_-_exempt_alaska_native_villages.md)      EXEMPT ALASKA NATIVE VILLAGES

- Updated "Exempt Alaska Native Villages" listing based on 2010 census data and a labor market survey conducted by Department of Labor (DOL) in 2020.

[ADDENDUM 2](../addenda/addendum_2_-_program_standards.md)      TA INCOME AND PROGRAM STANDARDS

- Updated the heating and non-heating utility standards for FFY 2021.

[ADDENDUM 3](../addenda/addendum_3_-_criteria_for_alaska_native_village_exemption.md)      CRITERIA FOR ALASKA NATIVE VILLAGE EXEMPTION FROM THE 60-MONTH TIME LIMIT

- Updated "Exempt Alaska Native Villages" listing based on 2010 census data and labor market survey conducted by [DOL](#)Department of Labor and Workforce Development in 2020.
