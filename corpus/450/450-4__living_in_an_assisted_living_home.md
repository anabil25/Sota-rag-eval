450-4        LIVING IN AN ASSISTED LIVING HOME

An assisted living home is a facility licensed by the state that provides residential care to persons who find it difficult or impossible to live by themselves. Care is provided in a residential environment and encourages independent living to the extent possible for each resident.

An individual who resides in an assisted living home for a full calendar month is subject to the Assisted Living Home payment standard. Refer to section [452-5D](../452/452-5_computation_of_benefits.md#452_5_D_ASSISTED_LIVING_HOME_PAYMENTS) for calculating the APAAdult Public Assistance payments for residents of assisted living homes. Refer to [Addendum 1](../addendum/addendum_1.md) for the need and maximum payment standards that apply to assisted living home residents.

Note:

A list of currently licensed Assisted Living Homes can be found on the [DHCS](#)Division of Health Care Services Assisted Living Licensing [website](https://health.alaska.gov/dhcs/Pages/cl/all/default.aspx).
