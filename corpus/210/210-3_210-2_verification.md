210-2        VERIFICATION

The factors of eligibility must be verified by the worker, and documented in the case record.  Verification of the client’s specific need and his financial situation will be necessary.  In some cases, verification of the age of minors and subsistence expenses (i.e. shelter costs) is also needed.  Evidence may be provided in the form of written verification, pertinent legal documents, or collateral contacts.

A GRAGeneral Relief Assistance payment to prevent an eviction or utility shut off requires that the vendor receiving the payment verify they will accept the GRAGeneral Relief Assistance payment in lieu of the eviction or utility shut off.  If the vendor does verify the payment will not prevent eviction or utility shut off, there is no GRAGeneral Relief Assistance eligibility.

Example 1:

If a person applies for rent money, he must produce an eviction notice or satisfy the eligibility worker of his inability to secure even temporary lodging, in addition to meeting the basic eligibility criteria (refer to [Section 220 -1B](../220/220-1_non-financial_eligibility.md#220-1_B._SPECIFIC_NEED) Specific Need).

Example 2:

 A person applying for money to pay an electric bill to prevent having his power shut off must produce a notice of termination from the utility company.  In the case of utility shut off, the Heating Assistance Program may be a prior resource for the household.

It is important to remember that GRAGeneral Relief Assistance is an emergency program.  A notice that an account is 30 days past due is not sufficient evidence of need, but a call to the electric company verifying the impending termination of service provides satisfactory verification of emergency need.  Any such statement must be documented in the case record.

Verification in villages serviced by a Fee Agent will be satisfied by a signed statement attesting to the validity of information given on the application.  Such a statement should be similar to:

"I am aware of this household's circumstances and attest to the best of my knowledge, that all information cited in this application for \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ is complete and accurate."

 \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_     \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

 (Fee Agent's Signature)            (Date)

Where there are compelling reasons for haste, a case can be opened and assistance granted prior to the receipt of all necessary documentation, provided there is no reason to doubt the statements or facts presented by the applicant.  Assistance in such cases will be granted upon approval by the Regional Manager or appointed designee.  The provision of assistance in such cases will not relieve the recipient of the liability to repay improperly granted assistance, nor will it preclude possible fraud prosecution.  The provision of assistance in such cases will not relieve the recipient of the liability to repay improperly granted assistance, client or agency caused, nor will it preclude possible fraud prosecution.
