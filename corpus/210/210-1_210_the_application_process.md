210            THE APPLICATION PROCESS

Any person may apply for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance regardless of age, race, religion, sex, or national origin.  Application will be made on a form designated by the Division of Public Assistance, available at any District Office or through authorized fee agents, and will be filed at the District Office.

The District Office must provide an application to a person upon request and must accept all identifiable applications submitted.  An identifiable application is an application or affidavit containing at least the legal name, address, date, and signature or witness mark of the applicant or his authorized representative (the person designated to act in the needy person’s behalf in the event of his mental or physical incapacity).  In order for assistance to be granted, however, all portions of the application must be completed.  Applicants should be instructed to enter N/A in sections of the application that do not pertain to their particular situations.

Applicants may submit an application for [GRA](#)General Relief Assistance by contacting the Division of Public Assistance by telephone and completing the telephonic application process.  The telephonic application process allows an applicant to complete and sign an acceptable application over the phone with [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance staff.

The Division of Public Assistance will accept applications for GRAGeneral Relief Assistance burial assistance that are submitted on a Tribal organization’s application form.
