125-2          MEDICAL
SCREENING PROCESS FOR INTERIM BLINDNESS OR DISABILITY DETERMINATION

To be eligible for IAInterim Assistance,
an individual must meet certain requirements including disability. The
applicant must appear likely to meet Social Security disability requirements
and be determined disabled by the Division. The APAAdult Public Assistance
program uses the same definitions of disability and blindness as SSISupplemental Security Income. The Division will obtain and use
medical and other information to determine whether an applicant is likely
to meet the SSISupplemental Security Income disability/blindness
criteria, and is therefore eligible for IAInterim Assistance.

The Medical Reviewer will assess the individual's
disability using the examination results reported on the AD 2Preliminary Examination for Interim Assistance Preliminary Examination for IAInterim Assistance form, request additional medical
evidence with the GEN 150Authorization for Release of Protected Health Information
forms provided, review any supporting documents or information, and make
a decision on whether the applicant appears to meet SSISupplemental Security Income
disability criteria.
