722-1          SUITABLE EMPLOYMENT

Suitable employment is any employment that meets the requirements of the Alaska Wage and Hour Act. This act provides for minimum wage and overtime standards for workers at levels consistent with their health, efficiency, and well-being. Essentially, this means that suitable employment must pay at least Alaska minimum wage, provide for overtime compensation, and not be any more hazardous to the health or well-being of the individual than to the average worker employed in a similar job.

Suitable employment does not include employment excluded by federal law. Non-suitable employment includes fields related to activities federally probihited, but allowed by Alaska state law, such as those within the cannabis industry. Should an individual choose to work in one of these fields, wages will be counted in the [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program budget; however, these hours are not countable toward work activities, and no work incentive deductions may be allowed.

If an individual refuses or quits non-suitable employment, no disqualification is imposed.
