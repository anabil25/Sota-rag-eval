STATE OF ALASKA

Department of Health and Social Services

Division of Public Assistance

TRANSMITTAL NUMBER:  2012-01

MANUAL:  General Relief Assistance

DATE:  March 1, 2012

We are making several changes to clarify existing policy as a result of suggestions from the field. 

Highlights to policy revisions are described below.  If you have any questions please contact the Policy and Program Development Team at 465-3382 or email [dpapolicy@alaska.gov](mailto:dpapolicy@alaska.gov).

OVERVIEW OF CHANGES

[MS 220-3(A)](../../220/income.md#220-3_A._UNEARNED_INCOME)  Unearned Income

1. - Deletes the last sentence in the first paragraph stating “No other deductions are allowed from unearned income”.  Since GRA eligibility is determined based on money that is actually available, if a portion of a person’s UIB is withheld for child support or the IRS, we would disregard the amount withheld.

[MS 220-3(E)](../../220/income.md#220-3_E._DEVELOPMENT_OF_INCOME)  Development of Income

1. - Adds a new section for development of income that states there is no development of income requirement for GRA.  This means there is no penalty if an applicant fails or refuses to apply for another assistance program (i.e. BIA-GA or TANF).

[MS 220-3(F)](../../220/income.md#220-3_F._SPECIAL_INCOME_PROVISIONS)  Special Income Provisions

1. - Adds a new section listing items that are not counted as income for GRA purposes including:  PFD, vendor payments and loans.

[MS 230](../../230/230_eligibility_determination.md)  Eligibility Determinations

1. - Corrects the first paragraph to read countable income must be lower than the need standard in order for the applicant to qualify for GRA.

[MS 230-1(B)](../../230/230-1_payments.md#230-1_B._BUDGETING_FOR_NEED_)  Budgeting for Need

1. - Changes the policy to match the regulations and EIS.  The regulations state the applicant is eligible if the income is under the need standard.  (EIS denies at $300).  If the income is equal to or higher than the need standard, the applicant is not eligible.

[MS 240-1](../../240/240-1_non-financial_eligibility.md)  Responsible Relatives and GRA Burial Services

1. - Adds a note to clarify that if a family applies for GRA Burial the month following the death of their family member, the surviving family members’ income and resources are not considered when determining eligibility since at no time in the month of application were they living with the deceased.

[MS 240-2(A)](../../240/240-2_specialized_burial_provisions.md#240-2_A._PUBLIC_ASSISTANCE_RECIPIENT_BURIALS)  Public Assistance Recipient Burials

1. - Adds a note to clarify trusts do not protect a deceased individual’s income from counting for burial assistance purposes.  When determining the deceased person’s eligibility for burial assistance, we must consider the individual’s monthly income before it is placed into the trust.

[MS 240-2(B)](../../240/240-2_specialized_burial_provisions.md#240-2_B._COURT_ORDERED_BURIALS)  Court Ordered Burials

1. - Adds a note to clarify although court ordered burials are always approved, the caseworker must review the burial charges and only pay those that are allowable as listed in GRA MS 240-3.

[MS 240-3(B)](../../240/240-3_payment.md#240-3_B._BASIC_SERVICES)  Basic Services

1. - Adds a note after #2 that states the $1250 includes casket rentals for viewing prior to cremation.

[MS 240-3(C)](../../240/240-3_payment.md#240-3_C._OTHER_SERVICES) Other Services

1. - Adds a note after #1 that states grave markers are part of the cost of a cemetery plot if they are required by the cemetery.  If the cemetery does not require grave markers, they are not an allowable expense.
   - Clarifies that cremation expenses include an urn and a special container used to house the body before cremation.

[MS 240-3(D)](../../240/240-3_payment.md#240-3_D._OUT-OF-STATE_PAYMENT_LIMITATIONS)  Out of State Payment Limitations

1. - Updates first paragraph that states GRA will pay out-of-state burial expenses if an individual was sent out of state for medical treatment authorized by Medicaid and then passed away.  Burial expenses may be paid at the place of death or, if requested, the deceased may be returned to Alaska for burial.

[MS 240-3(F)](../../240/240-3_payment.md#240-3_F._CONTRIBUTIONS)  Contributions

1. - Adds an example to clarify when a corporation contributes $500 toward a funeral, money is sent directly to the funeral home and is applied specifically toward expenses that are not covered by DPA, GRA payment to funeral home should not be reduced
