1007-14          DISQUALIFICATIONS

Disqualifications differ from penalties. Disqualifications result in someone or everyone in the household becoming ineligible to receive [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program benefits.

1007-14 A.      TYPES

Intentional Program Violations (IPV) are the result of a fraud investigation. See [ATAP MS 793-4](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/793/793-4_disqualification_for_intentional_program_violation.htm) for additional information. The case remains open and other family members continue to receive assistance.

Job Refusal or Job Quit results in the entire household becoming ineligible to receive ATAP benefits.  As a result, the case closes. See [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program [MS](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/ta.htm#t=710%2F710-3_disqualifying_conditions.htm) [710-3](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/ta.htm#t=710%2F710-3_disqualifying_conditions.htm), and [722-4](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/ta.htm#t=722%2F722-4_good_cause_for_job_refusal_or_job_quit.htm).

1007-14 B.      PARTICIPATION EXPECTATIONS

1. [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation - Clients serving an [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation must continue working with their case manager and must continue to adhere to Work Services participation requirements. Failure to develop a [FSSP](#)Family Self Sufficiency Plan, follow-through on activities, or cooperate with [CSSD](#)Child Support Services Division will result in a penalty. If the penalty is set before the [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation, the [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation is postponed until the penalty is resolved. If the [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation is set before the penalty, the [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation is suspended while the other penalty is in effect, see [ATAP MS 793-4 D](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/793/793-4_disqualification_for_intentional_program_violation.htm#793_4_d__imposing_an_ipv_disqualification_penalty) and [793-4 E](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/793/793-4_disqualification_for_intentional_program_violation.htm#793_4_e__the_ipv_disqualification_period). [CM](#)Case Managers must demonstrate due diligence in reaching out, engaging, and monitoring clients who are serving an [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation.

1. There are no participation expectations under a job refusal or job quit disqualification. The [ATAP](#)Alaska Temporary Assistance Program benefits are terminated during the disqualification period. The case cannot reopen until the disqualification period is complete. The case will be referred to Work Services if the family reapplies for assistance.

1007-14 C.      TIMEFRAME

1. [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation - An individual is disqualified from Temporary Assistance for intentional program violations for:

- Six months for the first violation;
- Twelve months for the second violation; and,
- Permanently for the third violation.

[IPV](#)Intentional Program Violations from other states or other programs are considered in determining the length of each [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation, see [ATAP MS 793-5 A](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/793/793-5_other_ipv_disqualification_information.htm#793_5_a__disqualifications_from_other_states_or_nfap).

1. Job Quit - An individual is disqualified for refusing or separating from suitable employment for:

- One month for the first refusal or separation;
- Six months for the second refusal or separation; and,
- Twelve months for the third and subsequent refusal or separation.

For the purpose of imposing the disqualification for refusing or separating from suitable employment, a month is 30 calendar days, see [ATAP MS 722-5](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/722/722-5_length_of_disqualification_for_job_quit_or_refusal_of_work.htm).

1007-14 D.      GOOD CAUSE

1. [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation - There is no good cause for an Intentional Program Violation.
2. Job Quit - The [CM](#)Case Manager will contact the employer to verify the terms under which the job ended or hours were reduced to determine if the change was within the client's control.  The [CM](#)Case Manager will determine if the client has Good Cause per [ATAP MS 723-1 C](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/723/723-1_good_cause.htm#723_1_c__good_cause_for_non_compliance_with_the_fssp_or_work_activities_requirements). If Good Cause is determined the change in employment and Good Cause Determination will be documented in [CLNO](#)Client Notes and uploaded through [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance's [Secure Document Portal](https://dpaforms.ilinx.com/ILINXFlex/public-eform?param=WEUwT0U5d0g5QTV2aFZiZlI2Z0VEdyUzRCUzRCAg). 

   If no Good Cause is identified, the [CM](#)Case Manager will enter a [CLNO](#)Client Notes, and [CANO](#)Case Note per the [Administrative Procedure Manual section 109-10](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/admin/apm.htm#t=109%2F109-10_case_managers_use_of_cano.htm) and upload the documentation through [DPA](#)Division of Public Assistance's [Secure Document Portal](https://dpaforms.ilinx.com/ILINXFlex/public-eform?param=WEUwT0U5d0g5QTV2aFZiZlI2Z0VEdyUzRCUzRCAg) requesting the Disqualification be imposed.

Clients will be encouraged by their [CM](#)Case Manager to call if they experience any difficulty while on the job. A [CM](#)Case Manager and/or job developer must be available to be in touch with the employer throughout a client's employment. Whenever possible, a Job Quit should be avoided by working with the employer to assist with additional training, accommodation, or assignment to alternate tasks.

1007-14 E.      RESOLVING

1. [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation - A change in the household composition may resolve the [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violation, see [ATAP MS 793-5 B](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/793/793-5_other_ipv_disqualification_information.htm#793_5_b__changes_in_household_composition).
2. Job Quit - Job Quit disqualifications continue after the case closes. When the appropriate amount of time has passed, the client can reapply for benefits. The case manager will discuss the circumstances under which the job ended and plan with the client for alternative choices in the future.

1007-14 F.      ENDING A DISQUALIFICATION

1. [IPV](#)Intentional Program Violations can only be appealed through a court of law, see [ATAP MS 793-3](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/793/793-3_administrative_disqualification_hearing.htm).
2. Job Quit disqualifications end if the person who incurred the Job Quit leaves the household, see [ATAP MS 722-8 B](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/722/722-8_other_job_quit_refusal_of_work_disqualification_information.htm#722_8_b__changes_in_household_composition), or the time period of the disqualification has been completed, see [ATAP MS 722-5](http://dpaweb.hss.state.ak.us/manuals/ta/722/722-5_length_of_disqualification_for_job_quit_or_refusal_of_work.htm).
